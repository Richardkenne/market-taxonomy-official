Part A — Business Domains

12 Business Domains

The twelve functional areas that organize how value is created and flows through any business — across any market, system, or era.

Foundation What Are Business Domains & Why 12? The structural basis, the sources, and the distinction from Business Systems.
→

Value creation

01 — Product & Innovation

Product Development & Innovation

Research, design, engineering, and continuous iteration. The primary source of differentiation and pricing power.

02 — Pricing & Revenue

Pricing & Revenue Optimization

Monetisation model, price level, and packaging. Often the highest-leverage lever in the business — affects margin directly.

03 — Operations

Operations & Execution

Manufacturing, delivery, and fulfillment processes. Controls cost structure, quality, and gross margin.

Market & growth

04 — Distribution

Market Access & Distribution

Channels, logistics, and reach infrastructure. Determines marginal unit economics and geographic scalability.

05 — Acquisition

Customer Acquisition & Growth

Sales, marketing, and top-line growth strategy. Drives CAC, revenue ramp, and market share. The growth engine.

06 — Retention

Customer Retention & Success

Support, success management, and churn reduction. Maximises LTV. In subscription models, often more profitable than acquisition.

Intelligence & moats

07 — Data & Insight

Data & Insight

Analytics, metrics, and decision intelligence. Turns raw data into strategic clarity. Can also become the product itself.

08 — Network Effects

Network Effects & Community

Two-sided dynamics, platform liquidity, and community engagement. The most durable moat — compounds over time.

09 — Brand

Brand & Market Position

Positioning, identity, and market perception. Strong brands command pricing premiums and reduce CAC through organic demand.

Organisation & capital

10 — Partnerships

Partnerships & Ecosystem

Strategic alliances, integrations, and platform relationships. Multiplies reach without proportional cost increases.

11 — People

People & Organisation

Hiring, culture, and team capability. The limiting factor in execution and innovation. Payroll is typically 50–70% of opex.

12 — Finance & Capital

Finance & Capital

Funding, cash management, and investor relations. Determines runway, growth capacity, and exit optionality.
