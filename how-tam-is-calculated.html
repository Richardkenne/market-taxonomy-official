The Total Addressable Market (TAM) values used in this system are indicative, global, and order-of-magnitude estimates. They are designed to support market comparison, prioritization, and strategic reasoning — not precise valuation or revenue forecasting.

Section 1

Separation of Market Structure and Market Size

This taxonomy explicitly separates market structure from market sizing.

  • Market structure — defined across Levels 0 to 3 — determines what markets exist and how they are organized.
  • Market size — expressed through TAM and SAM — estimates how much economic value may exist within those structures.
Conflating structure and size leads to authoritative-looking but misleading conclusions.

Section 2

Where TAM Applies in the Taxonomy

TAM is not applied uniformly across all levels.

Level TAM Role
Level 0 Total global size of the macro domain — defines the upper bound of economic reality.
Level 1 Structural allocation of economic activity across major categories — supports comparative analysis, not execution decisions.
Level 2 Addressable market base of a structurally recognized sub-market. Answers: is this market large enough to justify attention? This is the primary and correct level for TAM.
Level 3 TAM does not apply. See SAM below.

Section 3

Why TAM Stops at Level 2

Level 3 does not represent TAM. Level 3 introduces operational context — buyer type, usage pattern, scale, and institutionalization. At this level, market size becomes execution-dependent.

Applying TAM at Level 3 would mix market definition with monetization assumptions, which this system intentionally avoids.

Section 4

What Level 3 Represents Instead

At Level 3, market size should be interpreted as SAM — the Serviceable Available Market — or as a serviceable market slice expressed as a proportion of the Level 2 TAM.

This reflects the reality that businesses operate on specific, constrained segments rather than entire markets.

Section 5

Practical Usage

Level Use it to…
Level 2 TAM Decide whether a market is worth exploring.
Level 3 SAM Decide where and how a business can realistically be built.
TAM justifies attention. Level 3 justifies execution.

Methodological Note

How TAM Values Are Derived

TAM values are derived using market-appropriate sizing methods:

  • Asset-based approaches — total value of the relevant asset stock
  • Top-down market estimates — allocating a share of a global market to a structural sub-market
  • Bottom-up validation — multiplying potential buyers by maximum annual spend (used to validate top-down, not replace it)

No single method or source is treated as definitive. Ranges are preferred over point estimates.

Appendix — Technical Sizing Methodology

TAM Definition

TAM is an upper-bound estimate of total market value assuming full adoption within the structural market at Level 2. TAM is not expected revenue.

SAM at Level 3

At Level 3, market size is derived as a slice of Level 2 TAM. The addressable proportion reflects buyer type, geography, regulatory constraints, and operational feasibility.

What This System Avoids by Design

  • TAM definitions at Level 3
  • Single-source TAM claims
  • Precision without bounds
  • Revenue-only definitions of market size

Interpretation Rules

  • TAM values are ranges, not facts
  • Relative comparisons matter more than absolute numbers
  • Execution decisions should rely on SAM and feasibility, not TAM alone
