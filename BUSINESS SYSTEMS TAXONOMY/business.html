The 12 canonical systems — click Unit Economics to expand

01 — Subscription

Subscription

Recurring fee for continuous access. Predictable revenue, retention-driven. Examples: Netflix, Spotify, SaaS tools.

Unit: account / seat
LTV / CAC≥ 3×
Gross Margin≥ 70%
Net Revenue Retention≥ 100%
CAC Payback≤ 12 months
Logo Churn≤ 5% / yr (B2B)
If NRR < 100%, the business is leaking value faster than it acquires it.
02 — Transaction Fee

Transaction Fee

Commission charged per exchange facilitated. Revenue scales with volume. Examples: Stripe, PayPal, Airbnb.

Unit: transaction
Take rateSustainable for market
Net margin per txPositive
Volume × take rate> variable costs
Price elasticityLow
Volume concentrationAcceptable top-user mix
Take rate is the structural bet — too high kills supply, too low kills the business.
03 — Usage-Based

Usage-Based

Pay per unit consumed — compute, storage, API calls. No commitment, pure metering. Examples: AWS, Twilio, Snowflake.

Unit: unit consumed
Gross margin per unitStable
Marginal costsPredictable
Pricing vs perceived valueAligned
Revenue growth vs usageRevenue ≥ usage growth
Unbillable cost spikesNone
If usage grows faster than revenue, pricing is misaligned with cost structure.
04 — Licensing / IP

Licensing / IP

Rights to IP, brand, or technology rented to third parties. Asset-light, high-margin. Examples: Qualcomm, ARM, franchise brands.

Unit: licence
Gross Margin≥ 80%
RenewalsClear & frequent
Contractual enforcementReal
Ancillary service dependencyLow
Piracy / leakageControlled
High gross margin is the structural advantage — protect it by controlling enforcement and renewal cadence.
05 — Marketplace

Marketplace

Multi-sided intermediation connecting buyers and sellers. Network effects compound defensibility. Examples: Amazon, Uber, Etsy.

Unit: successful match
LiquidityAchievable locally
Take rateSustainable
CAC < LTVBoth sides
Transaction frequencyGrowing
Cold-startSolvable
A marketplace with no liquidity has no unit economics — solve cold-start before optimising take rate.
06 — Advertising / Attention

Advertising / Attention

Audience attention monetised by third-party advertisers. Scale and engagement are the moat. Examples: Google, Meta, YouTube.

Unit: impression / audience unit
CPM / CPC> audience cost
Monetisable inventorySufficient
Audience retentionStable
ARPUGrowing
3rd-party platform dependencyLimited
Audience is the asset — if retention declines, CPM falls and the entire unit economics inverts.
07 — Asset Ownership

Asset Ownership

Cash-flowing physical or financial assets held and operated. Returns from yield, appreciation, or utilisation. Examples: REITs, leasing, infrastructure.

Unit: asset
Net yieldPositive
Cashflow vs opexCF > operating costs
LeverageUnder control
Macro riskSustainable
Exit optionalityAvailable
If cashflow doesn't cover operating costs without leverage, the asset is not performing — it's speculating.
08 — Service / Retainer

Service / Retainer

Revenue from paid human expertise and execution. Project-based or ongoing retainer. Examples: consulting, agencies, law firms.

Unit: hour / project / client
Gross margin per hourPositive
Utilisation rate≥ 70%
Pricing modelNot purely time-based
Founder dependencyDeclining
Repeatable processesIn place
A service business where founder dependency isn't declining is not scalable — it's a job, not a system.
09 — Performance / Outcome

Performance / Outcome-Based

Paid only on results delivered — leads, conversions, AUM. Aligns incentives fully. Examples: affiliate networks, performance agencies, carried interest.

Unit: result achieved
DownsideLimited
UpsideUncapped
Risk-adjusted returnPositive
Deal flow accessStable
AsymmetryFavourable
The only viable outcome-based model is one where your cost base survives a run of non-conversions.
10 — Data / Insight

Data / Insight

Aggregated or analysed data sold as reports, feeds, or API access. Information itself is the product. Examples: Bloomberg, Nielsen, Palantir.

Unit: dataset / insight
Data collection costsAmortisable
Gross marginHigh
Data replicabilityHard to replicate
Update frequencyUseful cadence
Decisional valueReal
Data that can be easily replicated or scraped freely has no durable unit economics.
11 — Platform / Ecosystem

Platform / Ecosystem

A standard on which third parties build and monetise. Revenue from the layer below. Examples: Apple App Store, Salesforce, Android.

Unit: developer / app / integration
Core productAlready monetisable
Network effectsReal
GovernanceClear
Revenue shareSustainable
Switching costsGrowing
A platform with no core product monetisation is a subsidy programme, not a business.
12 — Hybrid / Stack

Hybrid / Stack

A stable, intentional combination of two or more systems above. Not an escape hatch — a structural outcome at scale. Examples: Amazon (01+02+05+07), Google (06+11).

Unit: composite system
Systems compatibilityConfirmed
Cross-subsidyIntentional
KPIsSeparable per system
ComplexityManageable
SynergiesReal, not assumed
A hybrid where KPIs cannot be separated per system is not a strategy — it's accounting opacity.
