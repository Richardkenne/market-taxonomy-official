Part A — Level 1
04 Agriculture

Agriculture markets are segmented by commodity type, production system, and input category — each with distinct seasonal dynamics, subsidy structures, and technology adoption curves.

We reference two types of sources: global classification standards used by agricultural agencies and trade bodies, and market-intelligence research used to size segments and track commodity and input trends. Use these to validate market definitions before narrowing to a specific crop system or geography.

How market categories are recognized How TAM is calculated
FAO — FAOSTAT Database USDA — Economic Research Service NAICS 11 — Agriculture, Forestry, Fishing & Hunting OECD — Agriculture & Fisheries Rabobank — Food & Agribusiness Research McKinsey — Agriculture

These categories follow NAICS 11 divisions and FAO commodity groupings — segmenting by production type (crops, livestock, aquaculture), input category, and technology layer.

01

Row Crops & Grain

Large-scale field crop production including corn, soybeans, wheat, and rice — driven by commodity pricing, yield optimization, and government subsidy programs.

Includes: Corn, Soybeans, Wheat, Rice, Cotton, Oilseeds.

 02

Livestock & Dairy

Animal husbandry operations spanning beef cattle, dairy farming, poultry, and swine — with distinct feed economics, animal welfare regulations, and processing chains.

Includes: Beef Cattle, Dairy, Poultry, Swine, Feed & Nutrition.

 03

Specialty Crops & Horticulture

High-value fruit, vegetable, nut, and greenhouse production requiring specialized labor, cold chain logistics, and shorter shelf-life management.

Includes: Fruits & Tree Nuts, Vegetables, Greenhouse & Nursery, Organic Production.

 04

Aquaculture & Fisheries

Farm-raised and wild-capture seafood production — including fish farming, shrimp cultivation, and marine harvest with sustainability certification requirements.

Includes: Fish Farming, Shrimp Aquaculture, Wild-Capture Fisheries, Seaweed & Algae.

 05

Agricultural Inputs & Services

Seeds, fertilizers, crop protection chemicals, and professional agronomic services that directly influence yield and cost-per-acre economics.

Includes: Seeds & Genetics, Fertilizers, Crop Protection, Agronomic Advisory, Soil Testing.

 06

AgTech & Precision Agriculture

Technology platforms for variable-rate application, remote sensing, farm management software, and IoT-connected equipment optimizing field-level decisions.

Includes: Precision Application, Remote Sensing & Drones, Farm Management Software, IoT Sensors, GPS Guidance.

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on segments with measurable yield or cost impact and fragmented operators. Examples: variable-rate fertilizer recommendation engines for Midwest corn growers; livestock health monitoring platforms for mid-size dairy operations; cold chain compliance tracking for specialty crop shippers.
