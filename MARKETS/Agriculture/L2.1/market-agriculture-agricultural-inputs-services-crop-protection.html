Crop Protection & Agrochemicals

The $75B+ crop protection market encompasses herbicides (42%), insecticides (27%), fungicides (25%), and other products that prevent yield losses from weeds, pests, and diseases. The industry faces a regulatory inflection point as key active ingredients (glyphosate, neonicotinoids, chlorpyrifos) face bans while biological alternatives grow at 12-15% CAGR.

The crop protection industry is at a crossroads: regulatory bans on legacy chemicals are accelerating while herbicide-resistant weeds now affect 70%+ of US soybean and cotton acres. Farmers face declining efficacy of existing products and limited new modes of action, creating demand for integrated pest management solutions that combine chemical, biological, and data-driven approaches.

Known Pain Points

  • Herbicide-resistant weeds now infest 70%+ of US soybean and cotton acres — Palmer amaranth costs US farmers $4B+ annually
  • Regulatory bans on key active ingredients (glyphosate reviews, neonicotinoid restrictions) threaten existing weed and pest management programs
  • Spray drift and off-target application waste 10-20% of chemical inputs and create liability risks
  • Pest scouting remains largely manual — farmers walk fields weekly rather than using real-time detection systems

How to Choose a Niche

The shift from chemical to integrated pest management creates whitespace for precision application technology (spot-spray rather than broadcast), AI-powered pest identification, and biological product efficacy platforms. Herbicide-resistant weed management is an urgent, high-value problem. Focus on the advisory/recommendation layer rather than competing with chemical manufacturers.
