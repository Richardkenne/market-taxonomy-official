Fish Farming (Finfish Aquaculture)

Farmed finfish production exceeds 57 million MT annually, led by carp, tilapia, salmon, catfish, and pangasius. Production systems range from extensive pond culture in Asia to intensive recirculating aquaculture systems (RAS) in Norway and Scotland. Salmon alone is a $20B+ market with 3M MT farmed annually.

Production data from FAO Fisheries and Aquaculture Department, NOAA aquaculture statistics, and Kontali Analyse salmon industry benchmarks.

Fish farming is the most feed-efficient animal protein system (1.1-1.5 FCR vs 6-8 for beef), yet disease outbreaks remain the #1 profitability risk. Sea lice alone cost the salmon industry $1B+ annually. The shift toward land-based RAS facilities is accelerating but capital costs are 5-10x higher than conventional cage farming.

Known Pain Points

  • Disease outbreaks (sea lice, ISA virus, EMS) cause $6B+ in annual losses industry-wide with limited early detection capability
  • Feed costs represent 50-70% of production expenses and depend heavily on volatile fishmeal/soybean prices
  • Environmental permitting for new ocean-based sites is increasingly restrictive, pushing toward costly land-based RAS
  • Water quality monitoring in open-water cages is intermittent rather than continuous, missing critical oxygen and temperature events

How to Choose a Niche

Salmon operations are the highest-value target — $5-7/kg farm-gate price with sophisticated operators willing to pay for technology. Fish health monitoring (computer vision, environmental sensing) has the most immediate ROI. Feed optimization is the largest cost lever. Avoid commodity species (tilapia, carp) initially — margins are too thin to support SaaS pricing.
