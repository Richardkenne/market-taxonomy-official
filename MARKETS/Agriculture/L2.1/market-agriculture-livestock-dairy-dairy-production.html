Dairy Production

Global milk production exceeds 930 million metric tons annually from 270 million dairy cows. Operations range from 5-cow subsistence farms to 50,000+ head mega-dairies, with rapid consolidation in developed markets driving herd sizes up while farm count declines 3-5% annually.

Industry structure from International Dairy Federation (IDF) World Dairy Situation report, USDA Dairy Data, and IFCN Dairy Research Network.

Dairy is uniquely data-rich: each cow produces milking data 2-3x daily, plus health, reproduction, and feed intake signals. Yet most operations use less than 20% of available data for decisions. The gap between data collection and actionable insight represents the core opportunity — especially as labor availability declines and herd sizes grow.

Known Pain Points

  • Mastitis (udder infection) costs the US dairy industry $2B+/year and is often detected too late for effective treatment
  • Reproduction management — missed heats and poor conception rates extend calving intervals by 30-60 days at $3-5/day cost per cow
  • Feed is 50-60% of total cost, but ration formulation rarely adjusts to actual forage quality variation within a season
  • Methane reporting and reduction requirements are emerging (EU, California) with no standardized measurement tools at farm level

How to Choose a Niche

Dairy is technology-forward compared to other livestock sectors, so differentiation matters. Mastitis early detection has clear ROI but established competition (DeLaval, Lely). Reproduction analytics and feed optimization have less competition. Methane measurement and reduction is early-stage but regulation is creating demand fast.
