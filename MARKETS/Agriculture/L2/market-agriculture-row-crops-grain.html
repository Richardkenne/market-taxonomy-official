Row Crops & Grain

Large-scale field crop production including cereals, oilseeds, and fiber crops grown in monoculture or rotation systems across millions of acres worldwide, forming the foundation of global food security and commodity trade.

Segments derived from USDA National Agricultural Statistics Service (NASS) crop classifications, FAO commodity groupings, and CBOT/CME commodity contract categories that define how row crops are traded and tracked globally.

Sources

USDA NASS FAO FAOSTAT CME Group USDA ERS

Row crops account for over 70% of global cropland. The sector is consolidating rapidly: the top 4 grain traders (ABCD — ADM, Bunge, Cargill, Louis Dreyfus) control roughly 70% of global grain trade, creating both bottlenecks and opportunities for technology-enabled disruption in logistics, pricing transparency, and farm-level decision-making.

How to Choose a Niche

Look for crops where information asymmetry creates pricing inefficiencies, or where post-harvest losses remain high (15-20% in developing nations). Specialty varieties within commodity crops (organic, non-GMO, identity-preserved) command premium pricing and have less efficient supply chains ripe for software solutions.
