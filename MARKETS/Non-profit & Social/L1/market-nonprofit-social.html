Part A — Level 1
41 Non-profit & Social

This market covers organizations pursuing public benefit missions through charitable services, advocacy, education, and social enterprise models funded by donations, grants, and earned revenue.

These sources classify non-profits by mission category, revenue source, and organizational size tracked by NCCS, Urban Institute, and philanthropic research bodies.

How market categories are recognized How TAM is calculated
NCCS — National Center for Charitable Statistics Giving USA — Charitable Giving Research NAICS 813 — Religious, Civic & Social Organizations Council on Foundations Candid — GuideStar Nonprofit Data

Segments are divided by mission type and funding model because operational structures, donor relationships, and technology needs differ substantially between human services organizations, foundations, advocacy groups, and social enterprises.

How to Choose a Niche

Select a mission category and funding model (grant-dependent vs. earned revenue vs. major gifts) before exploring operational or technology pain points — a food bank's inventory management needs are completely different from a foundation's grant-making workflow or an advocacy group's digital organizing tools. L2 niches include donation processing platforms, program impact measurement, and volunteer engagement technology.
