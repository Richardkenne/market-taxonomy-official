41 Non-profit & Social

Non-profit and social-impact markets are segmented by mission type, funding model, and organizational structure — each with distinct governance requirements, donor dynamics, and program delivery patterns.

We reference two types of sources: classification standards used by the IRS, NCCS, and international bodies to categorize tax-exempt organizations, and philanthropic research used to size giving markets and track nonprofit sector trends. Use these to validate mission categories before narrowing to a specific service area or funding model.

How market categories are recognized How TAM is calculated
NCCS — National Center for Charitable Statistics NAICS 813 — Religious, Grantmaking, Civic & Social Organizations IRS — Exempt Organizations Data (NTEE Codes) Giving USA — Annual Philanthropy Report Candid (GuideStar + Foundation Center) McKinsey Social Sector Practice

These categories follow the IRS National Taxonomy of Exempt Entities (NTEE) classification system and NAICS 813 divisions, reflecting how nonprofits are organized by mission focus, funding source, and operational model.

01

Human Services & Community Organizations

Social service agencies delivering direct assistance — food banks, homeless shelters, job training programs, and community development organizations serving vulnerable populations.

Includes: Food Banks, Emergency Shelters, Workforce Development, Community Action Agencies.

 02

Health & Medical Charities

Disease-focused foundations, hospital auxiliaries, and public health nonprofits funding medical research, patient support services, and health education campaigns.

Includes: Disease Research Foundations, Hospital Auxiliaries, Mental Health Nonprofits, Public Health Campaigns.

 03

Education & Youth Development

Scholarship foundations, after-school programs, literacy initiatives, mentoring organizations, and college access nonprofits serving K-12 and young adult populations.

Includes: Scholarship Funds, After-School Programs, Mentoring Networks, College Access Initiatives.

 04

Environmental & Animal Organizations

Conservation groups, environmental advocacy organizations, wildlife protection agencies, animal shelters, and humane societies focused on ecological and species welfare.

Includes: Land Conservation Trusts, Environmental Advocacy, Animal Shelters, Wildlife Protection.

 05

Foundations & Philanthropic Giving

Private foundations, community foundations, corporate giving programs, and donor-advised fund sponsors deploying capital through grants and impact investments.

Includes: Private Foundations, Community Foundations, DAF Sponsors, Corporate Philanthropy.

 06

Nonprofit Technology & Fundraising Platforms

Donor management CRMs, online fundraising tools, grant management software, volunteer coordination platforms, and impact measurement analytics built for mission-driven organizations.

Includes: Donor CRM, Online Fundraising, Grant Management, Volunteer Platforms, Impact Analytics.

How to Choose a Niche

Select a mission category and funding model (grant-dependent vs. earned revenue vs. major gifts) before exploring operational pain points. Examples: donor retention analytics for mid-size human services nonprofits; grant compliance reporting tools for community foundations; volunteer scheduling software for animal rescue networks; peer-to-peer fundraising platforms for disease-research charities.
