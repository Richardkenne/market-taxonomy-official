Part A — Level 2
Packaged & Processed Foods

Shelf-stable and manufactured food products spanning snacks, dairy, bakery, and preserved goods — the backbone of global grocery retail worth over $2 trillion annually.

Industry classification follows Euromonitor's packaged food taxonomy, NAICS 311 (Food Manufacturing), and IRI/Circana retail tracking categories used across CPG analytics.

Sources

Euromonitor Packaged Food IBISWorld NAICS 311 USDA Economic Research Mintel Food & Drink

Packaged foods face a dual squeeze: consumers demand cleaner labels and fewer additives, while input costs (commodities, packaging, logistics) keep climbing. Brands that master reformulation without sacrificing taste or shelf life capture outsized margin in an otherwise low-growth category.

How to Choose a Niche

Look for segments where consumer preferences are shifting faster than incumbent brands can adapt. Clean-label reformulation, portion control, and direct-to-consumer subscription models create defensible positions even against CPG giants. Prioritize niches where regulatory changes (sugar taxes, labeling laws) create compliance gaps you can fill.
