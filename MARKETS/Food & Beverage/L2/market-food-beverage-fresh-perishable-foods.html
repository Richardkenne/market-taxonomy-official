Part A — Level 2
Fresh & Perishable Foods

Fresh produce, dairy, meat, seafood, and bakery products requiring cold-chain logistics, rapid turnover, and stringent food safety controls throughout the supply chain.

Segments follow FAO commodity classifications, USDA ERS food availability data, and Rabobank fresh food supply chain analysis — covering the full farm-to-retail perishable value chain.

Sources

FAO FAOSTAT USDA Food Availability Rabobank Fresh Produce FDA FSMA

Fresh and perishable foods represent over $4 trillion globally, with 30-40% food waste rates making it one of the most inefficient segments in the food industry. FDA FSMA traceability requirements (Rule 204, effective 2026) are forcing digitization across the entire fresh food supply chain.

How to Choose a Niche

Fresh food is where the biggest operational pain points live — waste reduction, cold-chain monitoring, and traceability software have strong PMF. Target the produce supply chain (most fragmented) or seafood traceability (strongest regulatory tailwinds from FSMA 204 and EU IUU regulations). Avoid commodity trading — margins are thin and incumbents are entrenched.
