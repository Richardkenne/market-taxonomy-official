Alcoholic Beverages

Beer, wine, spirits, and ready-to-drink cocktails — a $500B+ industry navigating premiumization, craft movements, changing demographics, and the rise of no/low-alcohol alternatives.

The alcohol industry faces a generational shift: Gen Z drinks 20% less than millennials did at the same age. This is not just a trend — it is a structural demographic headwind. The opportunity lies in serving moderation-oriented consumers (no/low-alcohol, RTD, premium-less-volume) and helping traditional producers adapt their portfolios and distribution models.

Known Pain Points

  • Three-tier distribution complexity — in the US, mandatory producer-distributor-retailer separation creates enormous inefficiency and limits DTC models for craft producers
  • Age verification for e-commerce — online alcohol sales require identity verification at purchase and delivery, adding friction and cost that limits digital channel growth
  • Premiumization vs. volume decline — consumers buy less but pay more, requiring brands to fundamentally restructure margins and production volumes
  • Regulatory fragmentation — alcohol laws vary not just by country but by state/province, making multi-market expansion extraordinarily complex for small producers

How to Choose a Niche

Alcohol is heavily regulated, which creates moats for those who understand compliance. The best niches solve distribution pain (helping craft producers reach consumers), enable premiumization (brand storytelling, direct relationships), or serve the moderation movement (no/low-alcohol alternatives with authentic flavor profiles).
