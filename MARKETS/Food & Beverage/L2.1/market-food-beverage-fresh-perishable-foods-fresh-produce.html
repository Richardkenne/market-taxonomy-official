Fresh Produce

Fruits, vegetables, herbs, and salad mixes — from farm-gate through wholesale markets, distributors, and retail. The most fragmented and waste-intensive perishable food category.

Data sourced from FAO fresh produce trade data, USDA ERS Fruit and Vegetable Outlook, and Rabobank Fresh Produce research.

Market Context

Size / Scale~$1.2T global market; fruits ~$600B, vegetables ~$500B, fresh-cut/salads ~$100B
Growth signalFresh-cut and organic produce growing 7-9% CAGR; conventional produce 2-3%
Key playersDole, Fresh Del Monte, Driscoll’s, Total Produce (Dole plc), Costa Group, Greenyard
GeographyGlobal — China is largest producer; US, EU largest import markets
Fresh produce has the highest food waste rate of any category — 30-45% is lost between farm and consumer. The supply chain involves 5-8 intermediaries with limited visibility, making it the ripest F&B segment for technology disruption.

Known Pain Points

  • Food waste at 30-45% — lack of demand visibility, cold chain breaks, and cosmetic standards drive massive losses throughout the supply chain
  • Price discovery opacity — wholesale produce pricing is still largely phone-based with limited transparency, disadvantaging smaller buyers and sellers
  • Traceability gaps — FDA FSMA 204 requires lot-level tracking for leafy greens and other high-risk produce by January 2026
  • Labor-intensive harvesting — fresh produce relies on manual picking with 20-30% labor cost increases in recent years

How to Choose a Niche

Produce traceability software has the strongest regulatory tailwind (FSMA 204). Wholesale produce marketplace platforms connecting growers directly to buyers have proven traction. Waste reduction analytics for produce distributors have clear ROI.
