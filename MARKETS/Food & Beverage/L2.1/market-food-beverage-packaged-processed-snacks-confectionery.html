Snack Foods & Confectionery

The global snack and confectionery market encompasses salty snacks, chocolate, sugar confectionery, gum, and better-for-you alternatives — one of the fastest-growing and most innovation-driven packaged food categories.

Segment boundaries follow Euromonitor's Sweet Biscuits, Snack Bars, and Savoury Snacks categories, cross-referenced with IRI/Circana retail scan data and Mondelez International's category definitions.

GeographyNorth America 35%, Europe 28%, Asia-Pacific 25% — fastest growth in India and SE Asia
The snacking occasion has replaced traditional meal structures for many consumers. The real opportunity lies not in creating another chip brand, but in solving the infrastructure problems: helping manufacturers reformulate at speed, manage SKU complexity across regional taste preferences, and navigate the growing patchwork of sugar/salt regulations across markets.

Known Pain Points

  • Reformulation pressure — reducing sugar, salt, and artificial ingredients without sacrificing taste or shelf life, while meeting different regulatory thresholds per country
  • SKU proliferation and complexity — managing hundreds of regional variants, limited editions, and format sizes across fragmented retail channels
  • Sustainable packaging mandates — transitioning from single-use plastics to recyclable or compostable packaging without compromising product freshness
  • DTC and e-commerce fulfillment — snack products have low unit economics for shipping; breakage, freshness, and last-mile costs erode margins in online channels

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on where consumer behavior is changing fastest — meal replacement snacking, functional ingredients in familiar formats, or regional snack brands going global. The best niches sit at the intersection of a clear consumer trend and an operational bottleneck that incumbents are too slow to solve.
