Seafood & Aquaculture

Wild-caught fish, farmed shellfish, and aquaculture products — the most globally traded perishable food category with complex chain-of-custody and sustainability certification requirements.

Market Context

Size / Scale~$280B global market; aquaculture ~$160B (57% of production), wild-caught ~$120B
Growth signalAquaculture growing 5-7% CAGR; wild-caught flat; sustainable seafood premium growing
Key playersThai Union, Mowi, Maruha Nichiro, Nippon Suisan, Cermaq, Charoen Pokphand Foods
GeographyGlobal — China produces 35%+ of global aquaculture; Norway, Chile lead salmon farming
Seafood is the most internationally traded food commodity — over 38% of production crosses borders. IUU (illegal, unreported, unregulated) fishing accounts for up to 26 million tonnes annually, driving regulatory demand for traceability from boat to plate.

Known Pain Points

  • IUU fishing and fraud — up to 30% of seafood imports may be mislabeled or illegally caught, creating regulatory and reputational risk
  • Aquaculture disease management — fish disease outbreaks can destroy entire farm harvests with limited real-time monitoring tools
  • Cold chain integrity — seafood requires 0-4°C maintenance with a 5-7 day fresh shelf life, making logistics critical
  • Sustainability certification complexity — MSC, ASC, BAP, and other standards each have different audit requirements and chain-of-custody rules

How to Choose a Niche

Seafood traceability platforms (boat-to-plate) have the strongest regulatory and buyer demand. Aquaculture farm management software is growing rapidly in Norway and Chile. Avoid commodity fish trading — build the data infrastructure layer instead.
