Coffee & Tea

Roasted coffee, specialty single-origin, instant coffee, loose-leaf and bagged tea, RTD coffee, and coffee capsule/pod systems — one of the fastest premiumizing beverage categories globally.

Data sourced from Euromonitor Hot Drinks reports, ICO (International Coffee Organization) trade data, and Specialty Coffee Association market research.

Market Context

Size / Scale~$320B global market; retail coffee ~$140B, coffee shops ~$100B, tea ~$80B
Growth signalSpecialty coffee growing 8-10% CAGR; RTD coffee +12%; capsules +7%
Key playersNestlé (Nespresso), JDE Peet’s, Starbucks, Lavazza, Tata Global, Keurig Dr Pepper
GeographyGlobal — US, EU, Japan largest; China fastest-growing coffee market (25% CAGR)
Coffee is undergoing a premiumization wave comparable to wine — single-origin, direct trade, and specialty roasting command 3-5x price premiums. Meanwhile, tea innovation is accelerating through functional blends, matcha, and RTD formats.

Known Pain Points

  • Green coffee sourcing complexity — traceability from farm to cup involves 6-8 intermediaries with limited transparency on quality and pricing
  • Coffee shop labor and training — barista turnover exceeds 100% annually in most markets, creating quality inconsistency
  • Capsule/pod sustainability — single-serve formats face environmental backlash despite consumer convenience preference
  • Tea authentication — premium teas face adulteration and mislabeling, with limited tools for origin verification

How to Choose a Niche

Coffee supply chain traceability platforms and specialty coffee sourcing marketplaces have strong traction. Coffee shop management software (beyond POS) — inventory, recipe, training — is underserved. Avoid generic coffee e-commerce — the market is saturated.
