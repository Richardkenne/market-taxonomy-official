Coffee & Tea

Roasted coffee, specialty single-origin, tea (loose leaf, bags, matcha, RTD), and coffee pods/capsules — a $200B+ category driven by third-wave coffee culture, premium tea, and at-home brewing innovation.

Segment follows ICO (International Coffee Organization) trade categories and Euromonitor's Hot Drinks taxonomy, supplemented by Specialty Coffee Association market reports and Tea Association data.

Sources

Intl. Coffee Organization Specialty Coffee Assoc. Euromonitor Hot Drinks Tea Association of USA

Market Context

Size / Scale$200B+ globally (coffee ~$130B retail, tea ~$70B)
Growth signalSpecialty coffee 12% CAGR; coffee pods/capsules 8%; matcha/premium tea 10%+
Key playersNestlé (Nespresso), Starbucks, JDE Peet's, Lavazza, Tata Consumer, Unilever (Lipton)
GeographyEurope 35% (coffee), Asia 55% (tea), North America 25% — China fastest-growing specialty coffee market
Coffee and tea are unique in food & beverage: they combine commodity trading dynamics (coffee is the second most traded commodity after oil) with luxury brand positioning (single-origin, estate-grown). The structural tension between volatile commodity costs and consumers' willingness to pay $6+ for a specialty latte creates both risk and opportunity throughout the value chain.

Known Pain Points

  • Climate change impact on coffee supply — arabica coffee growing regions are shrinking due to rising temperatures, threatening 50% of current production area by 2050
  • Pod/capsule waste and sustainability — Nespresso-style capsules generate massive plastic/aluminum waste; recycling programs remain ineffective at scale
  • Fair trade and supply chain transparency — consumers demand ethical sourcing but traceability from smallholder farmers to retail is fragmented and hard to verify
  • At-home vs. out-of-home channel shift — post-pandemic at-home consumption patterns persist, disrupting traditional coffeehouse-centric business models

How to Choose a Niche

Coffee and tea reward vertical integration and storytelling. Specialty coffee niches favor those with sourcing relationships and roasting expertise. Tea niches favor cultural knowledge and premiumization skill. Technology niches (smart brewing, pod sustainability) favor engineering talent. Pick based on where in the value chain you can add the most defensible value.
