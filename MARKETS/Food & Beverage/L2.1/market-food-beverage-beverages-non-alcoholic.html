Non-Alcoholic Beverages

Carbonated soft drinks, bottled water, juices, RTD teas, and sports drinks — the largest beverage segment by volume, undergoing rapid sugar-reduction and premiumization.

Data sourced from Euromonitor Soft Drinks tracker, Beverage Marketing Corporation industry reports, and USDA ERS beverage expenditure data.

Market Context

Size / Scale~$950B global market; bottled water ~$350B, CSD ~$280B, juice ~$180B
Growth signalBottled water growing 6-8% CAGR; CSD flat to declining; flavored/sparkling water booming
Key playersCoca-Cola, PepsiCo, Nestlé Waters, Danone, Keurig Dr Pepper, Suntory
GeographyGlobal — US, China, Japan, EU are largest markets
The non-alcoholic beverage industry is in the middle of a structural shift away from sugar-heavy drinks toward water, sparkling water, and functional options. Sugar taxes in 40+ countries are accelerating reformulation.

Known Pain Points

  • Sugar reduction without taste compromise — reformulating legacy drinks to meet consumer and regulatory demands while maintaining brand loyalty
  • Packaging sustainability pressure — single-use plastic bans driving transition to rPET, aluminum, and refill systems with higher costs
  • DSD (direct store delivery) route optimization — beverage distribution relies on complex route delivery networks needing real-time optimization
  • Vending and cooler placement analytics — brands compete for limited cold-drink placement at retail with limited data on ROI

How to Choose a Niche

Beverage route optimization and distribution management software has proven PMF with mid-market distributors. Reformulation tools for sugar reduction are emerging. Avoid launching a new water brand — the market is saturated at the consumer level.
