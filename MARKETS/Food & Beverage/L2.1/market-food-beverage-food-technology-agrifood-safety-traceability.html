GeographyGlobal — US (FSMA) and EU (General Food Law) drive regulatory demand
Food safety traceability is experiencing a once-in-a-generation regulatory tailwind — FDA FSMA Rule 204 requires lot-level traceability for high-risk foods by January 2026, affecting an estimated 50,000+ food businesses that must implement digital tracking systems.

Known Pain Points

  • FSMA 204 compliance deadline — 50,000+ food businesses must implement lot-level traceability by January 2026 with limited guidance on technology requirements
  • Data interoperability — supply chain traceability requires data exchange between dozens of systems (ERP, WMS, TMS) that use different formats and standards
  • Recall response time — FDA expects 24-hour recall capability but most food companies still take 3-7 days to trace affected products
  • Cost of compliance — small and mid-size food businesses face $50-500K implementation costs for traceability systems with uncertain ROI beyond compliance

How to Choose a Niche

FSMA 204 traceability software targeting the 50,000+ affected businesses is the single best opportunity in food technology right now. Focus on mid-market food manufacturers and distributors (50-500 employees) underserved by enterprise solutions like SAP. Recall management automation has clear ROI beyond compliance.
