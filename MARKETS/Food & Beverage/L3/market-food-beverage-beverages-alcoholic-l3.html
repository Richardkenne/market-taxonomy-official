Craft & Independent Spirits

Small-batch gin, whiskey, rum, and vodka from independent distilleries — over 2,700 craft distilleries in the US alone, growing 20%+ annually but struggling with distribution access and brand visibility against multinational portfolios.

No & Low-Alcohol Alternatives

Non-alcoholic beer, wine, spirits, and RTD cocktails designed for sober-curious consumers — a $13B+ segment growing 7% annually, led by Athletic Brewing, Seedlip, and Heineken 0.0 as moderation becomes mainstream.

RTD Cocktails & Hard Seltzers

Ready-to-drink canned cocktails and hard seltzers — convenience-driven formats growing 15% CAGR, attracting spirits brands (Diageo's Smirnoff Ice, High Noon) and disrupting traditional on-premise cocktail culture.

How to Choose a Niche

Craft spirits reward brand building and local distribution knowledge. No/low-alcohol is a secular trend with demographic tailwinds but requires formulation innovation. RTD cocktails reward manufacturing scale and retail placement. All three niches face heavy regulation — your advantage comes from understanding compliance as a moat, not a barrier.
