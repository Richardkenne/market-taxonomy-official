Process Manufacturing

Continuous and batch production of goods through chemical reactions, mixing, and formulation — where raw materials are transformed into products that cannot be disassembled back into their components.

Segments derived from American Chemistry Council industry data, ISPE pharmaceutical manufacturing guidelines, and NAICS codes 325 (Chemical), 311 (Food), and 3254 (Pharmaceutical) covering the core process manufacturing verticals.

Sources

American Chemistry Council ISPE Pharma Manufacturing McKinsey Chemicals ISM Manufacturing

Process manufacturing accounts for roughly 40% of global manufacturing output. These industries face unique challenges around batch traceability, regulatory compliance (FDA, EPA, REACH), and the inherent difficulty of controlling continuous chemical and biological processes where a single deviation can spoil entire production runs worth millions.

How to Choose a Niche

Process manufacturers value solutions that reduce batch failures, ensure regulatory compliance, and optimize yield. Chemical plants prioritize safety and emissions monitoring. Food processors need traceability from ingredient to shelf. Pharma manufacturers will pay premium prices for validated systems that reduce deviation investigations and accelerate batch release.
