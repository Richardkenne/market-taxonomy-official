Industrial Machinery & Equipment

Design and production of capital equipment used across all manufacturing sectors — machine tools, material handling systems, pumps, compressors, and specialized industrial machinery.

Segments derived from AMT (Association for Manufacturing Technology) classifications, VDMA German engineering data, and NAICS 333 (Machinery Manufacturing) sub-categories covering machine tools, material handling, and fluid power equipment.

The global industrial machinery market exceeds $600B and is driven by automation demand, reshoring investments, and aging equipment replacement cycles. US machine tool consumption alone reached $9.4B in 2024 (AMT), while aftermarket parts and services often exceed 60% of OEM lifetime revenue.

How to Choose a Niche

Industrial machinery OEMs increasingly compete on service and uptime guarantees rather than hardware specs alone. Solutions for predictive maintenance, remote machine monitoring, and aftermarket parts management address the industry's shift toward servitization. Machine tool shops are highly fragmented (80%+ have fewer than 50 employees), making them accessible SaaS targets.
