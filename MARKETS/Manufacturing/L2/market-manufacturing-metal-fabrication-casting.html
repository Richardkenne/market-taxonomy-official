Metal Fabrication & Casting

Shaping raw metals into finished parts through cutting, bending, welding, casting, and forging — the backbone of industrial supply chains serving automotive, construction, aerospace, and energy sectors.

Segments derived from FMA (Fabricators & Manufacturers Association) industry classifications, AFS (American Foundry Society) casting data, and AISC structural steel statistics covering the major metal forming and fabrication verticals.

The US metal fabrication industry generates over $400B in annual revenue across 55,000+ establishments, with 90% being small businesses under 50 employees (Census Bureau). The sector faces acute skilled welder shortages — AWS projects a deficit of 400,000 welders by 2028 — driving demand for automation and training solutions.

How to Choose a Niche

Metal fabrication shops are highly fragmented and traditionally underserved by software. Quoting and estimating is still largely manual (spreadsheets or tribal knowledge). Job tracking, material nesting optimization, and weld quality documentation are high-value pain points. Foundries face additional environmental compliance and energy cost pressures that create technology opportunities.
