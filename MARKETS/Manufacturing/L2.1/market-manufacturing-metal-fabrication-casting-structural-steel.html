Structural Steel Fabrication

Fabrication of structural steel beams, columns, trusses, and connections for commercial buildings, bridges, industrial facilities, and infrastructure projects per AISC standards.

Sources used to define and size this segment within the manufacturing taxonomy.

Sources

AISC Steel Construction NASFA Steel Fabricators STRUCTURE Magazine ENR Construction

Market Context

Size / Scale~$140B global structural steel fabrication
Growth signal3-5% CAGR driven by commercial construction recovery and infrastructure spending
Key playersNucor, Steel Technologies, Cives Steel, Herrick Corp, DBM Global
GeographyUS (20%), China (35%), Europe (15%), India (10%)
Structural steel fabrication is project-based manufacturing with unique challenges — every building is different, requiring custom detailing, fabrication sequences, and erection plans. BIM adoption is transforming the industry but integration between design models and shop floor execution remains poor.

Known Pain Points

  • BIM-to-fabrication handoff loses critical information, requiring manual re-entry of connection details and material specifications
  • Project scheduling across multiple simultaneous jobs with shared equipment creates constant capacity conflicts
  • Erection sequence planning and piece tracking from shop to job site relies on manual systems at most fabricators

How to Choose a Niche

Mid-size structural fabricators ($10M-$100M revenue) running 3-10 simultaneous projects have the most scheduling pain — large enough for complexity, small enough to lack integrated project management.
