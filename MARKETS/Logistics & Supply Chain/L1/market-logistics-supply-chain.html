36 Logistics & Supply Chain

Logistics & Supply Chain markets are segmented by transport mode, cargo type, and service model — each with distinct operational complexity, asset intensity, and technology adoption patterns.

We reference two types of sources: global classification standards used by trade and statistical agencies, and market-intelligence research used to size logistics segments and track freight and supply chain trends. Use these to validate market definitions before narrowing to a specific transport mode or geography.

These categories follow NAICS 48-49 transport divisions and CSCMP supply chain framework — organized by physical movement stage, from long-haul freight through last-mile delivery and upstream procurement.

01

Freight & Cargo Transportation

Ocean, air, rail, and trucking services moving goods across long-haul domestic and international trade lanes.

Includes: Ocean Freight, Air Cargo, Rail Freight, Full Truckload, Less-Than-Truckload.

 02

Warehousing & Fulfillment

Storage facilities, distribution centers, cold chain infrastructure, and e-commerce fulfillment operations managing inventory and order processing.

Includes: Distribution Centers, Cold Storage, 3PL Fulfillment, Micro-Fulfillment.

 03

Last-Mile Delivery

Final-leg delivery from distribution hubs to consumers and businesses, including parcel, courier, and same-day delivery networks.

Includes: Parcel Delivery, Courier Services, Same-Day Delivery, Locker Networks.

 04

Supply Chain Management & Procurement

Strategic sourcing, demand planning, inventory optimization, and supplier relationship management across multi-tier supply networks.

Includes: Demand Planning, Strategic Sourcing, Procurement Platforms, Inventory Optimization.

 05

Customs, Trade Compliance & Freight Forwarding

Customs brokerage, trade documentation, duty management, and freight forwarding services enabling cross-border goods movement.

Includes: Customs Brokerage, Trade Compliance Software, Freight Forwarding, Duty Management.

 06

Supply Chain Technology & Visibility

Transportation management systems, warehouse management software, real-time tracking platforms, and supply chain analytics tools.

Includes: TMS, WMS, Real-Time Visibility Platforms, Supply Chain Analytics.

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on segments with route complexity, carrier fragmentation, or compliance burden where manual processes create delays. Examples: freight audit and payment automation for shippers managing 50+ carriers; real-time container tracking for importers with multi-port supply chains; last-mile route optimization for regional delivery fleets with 100+ daily stops.
