Part A — Level 1
28 Education

Education markets are segmented by learner stage, delivery model, and funding source — each with distinct accreditation requirements, outcome metrics, and technology adoption patterns.

We reference two types of sources: global classification standards used by education ministries and accreditation bodies, and market-intelligence research used to size segments and track enrollment and spending trends. Use these to validate market definitions before narrowing to a specific learner segment or geography.

How market categories are recognized How TAM is calculated

These categories follow UNESCO's ISCED framework for learner stages, cross-referenced with NAICS 61 service divisions and HolonIQ's EdTech taxonomy for technology-enabled delivery models.

01

Early Childhood Education

Pre-primary programs for children ages 0-5, including daycare centers, preschools, and kindergarten readiness services funded by a mix of public subsidies and parent tuition.

Includes: Daycare Centers, Preschools, Kindergarten Programs, Parent-Toddler Classes.

 02

K-12 Education

Primary and secondary schooling systems covering grades K through 12, including public districts, private schools, and charter networks with curriculum and assessment mandates.

Includes: Public School Districts, Private Schools, Charter Networks, Curriculum Providers.

 03

Higher Education

Universities, community colleges, and degree-granting institutions operating on tuition, endowment, and research-grant revenue models with complex accreditation requirements.

Includes: Universities, Community Colleges, Graduate Programs, Research Institutions.

 04

Vocational & Trade Education

Skills-based training programs leading to certifications and apprenticeships in trades such as healthcare, construction, IT, and manufacturing.

Includes: Trade Schools, Apprenticeships, Certification Programs, Technical Institutes.

 05

Corporate Training & L&D

Enterprise learning and development programs including compliance training, leadership development, and workforce upskilling funded by employer budgets.

Includes: Compliance Training, Leadership Programs, Skills Bootcamps, LMS Platforms.

 06

Online & EdTech Platforms

Digital-first learning platforms delivering courses, tutoring, and assessment tools via web and mobile — including MOOCs, adaptive learning engines, and virtual classrooms.

Includes: MOOCs, Adaptive Learning, Online Tutoring, Virtual Classrooms, Assessment Tools.

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on segments with measurable outcomes and recurring enrollment or training cycles. Examples: student information systems for K-12 districts managing attendance and grading; AI-powered tutoring platforms for test prep in higher-ed admissions; compliance training automation for enterprise L&D teams with audit requirements.
