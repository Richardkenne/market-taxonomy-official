Part A — Level 1
08 Automotive

Automotive markets are segmented by vehicle type, value chain position, and ownership model — each with distinct manufacturing economics, regulatory exposure, and electrification dynamics.

We reference two types of sources: global classification standards used by automotive regulators and trade associations, and market-intelligence research used to size segments and track production and sales trends. Use these to validate market definitions before narrowing to a specific vehicle segment or geography.

How market categories are recognized How TAM is calculated
NAICS 3361-3363 — Motor Vehicle Manufacturing OICA — International Automobile Manufacturers SAE International — Automotive Standards S&P Global Mobility McKinsey — Automotive & Assembly IEA — Global EV Data Explorer

These categories follow NAICS 336 divisions and GICS Automobiles & Components sub-industry structure — segmenting by vehicle class (passenger, commercial), value chain role (OEM, parts, retail, aftermarket), and powertrain transition (ICE to EV).

01

Passenger Vehicle OEM

Design, assembly, and sale of passenger cars and light trucks by original equipment manufacturers — driven by platform economics, emissions standards, and model cycle planning.

Includes: Sedans & Hatchbacks, SUVs & Crossovers, Light Trucks, Luxury Vehicles, Platform Engineering.

 02

Commercial Vehicle Manufacturing

Production of medium and heavy-duty trucks, buses, and specialty vehicles — with fleet procurement cycles, vocational configurations, and uptime-driven economics.

Includes: Heavy-Duty Trucks, Medium-Duty Trucks, Buses & Coaches, Specialty & Vocational Vehicles.

 03

Automotive Parts & Components

Tier 1-3 suppliers of powertrain, chassis, electronics, and interior systems — with JIT delivery requirements and OEM qualification processes.

Includes: Powertrain Components, Chassis & Suspension, Automotive Electronics, Interior Systems, Tires.

 04

Automotive Retail & Dealerships

New and used vehicle sales channels including franchise dealerships, online platforms, and fleet sales — with F&I revenue and trade-in economics.

Includes: Franchise Dealerships, Used Vehicle Sales, Online Retail, Fleet Sales, F&I Products.

 05

Aftermarket & Vehicle Services

Post-sale maintenance, repair, parts distribution, and vehicle servicing — spanning independent shops, dealer service centers, and quick-lube chains.

Includes: Independent Repair, Dealer Service, Parts Distribution, Collision Repair, Car Wash & Detailing.

 06

Electric Vehicles & Mobility Tech

Battery electric and plug-in hybrid vehicle production, charging infrastructure, battery technology, and connected vehicle software platforms.

Includes: BEV Manufacturing, Charging Networks, Battery Cells & Packs, V2X Connectivity, Autonomous Driving.

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on segments with recurring transactions and fragmented operators. Examples: shop management software for independent repair garages; digital retailing and F&I workflow tools for franchise dealership groups; EV charger site selection and permitting platforms for charging network operators.
