Part A — Level 1
11 Retail & E-commerce

This market covers the sale of goods and services to end consumers through physical stores, online channels, and omnichannel formats.

These sources classify retail by channel format, merchandise category, and transaction volume tracked by the U.S. Census, NRF, and e-commerce platforms.

How market categories are recognized How TAM is calculated
U.S. Census — Monthly Retail Trade Survey National Retail Federation (NRF) NAICS 44-45 — Retail Trade eMarketer — E-commerce & Retail Shopify Commerce Trends Report

Segments are divided by channel format and merchandise type because store economics, technology stacks, and customer acquisition strategies differ fundamentally between brick-and-mortar, pure-play e-commerce, and marketplace formats.

How to Choose a Niche

Start by defining the channel (online vs. in-store vs. omni) and category before exploring technology or operations pain points — inventory shrinkage, cart abandonment, and loyalty economics are channel-specific problems. L2 niches include personalization engines, returns management, and supply-chain visibility for retailers.
