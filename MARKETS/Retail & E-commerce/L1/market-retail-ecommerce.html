Part A — Level 1
11 Retail & E-commerce

Retail and e-commerce markets are segmented by channel format, merchandise category, and fulfillment model — each with distinct store economics, customer acquisition strategies, and supply-chain requirements.

We reference two types of sources: government retail trade statistics tracking sales volumes by store type and channel, and retail industry research used to size segments and forecast e-commerce penetration. Use these to validate market definitions before narrowing to a specific format or merchandise vertical.

01

E-commerce & Online Retail

Direct-to-consumer brands, online marketplaces, and digitally-native retailers selling across product categories through web and mobile storefronts.

Includes: Marketplace Platforms, DTC Brands, Social Commerce, Subscription E-commerce.

 02

General Merchandise & Department Stores

Big-box retailers, warehouse clubs, and department stores selling broad product assortments across apparel, home, electronics, and everyday essentials.

Includes: Department Stores, Warehouse Clubs, Discount Retailers.

 03

Grocery & Convenience Retail

Supermarkets, convenience stores, and food-drug-mass retailers selling perishable and packaged goods with high purchase frequency and thin margins.

Includes: Supermarkets, Convenience Stores, Online Grocery & Delivery.

 04

Specialty Retail

Category-focused chains in apparel, electronics, home improvement, sporting goods, and health/beauty — where deep assortment and expert service drive differentiation.

Includes: Apparel & Fashion, Electronics & Appliances, Home Improvement.

 05

Luxury & Premium Retail

High-end fashion houses, jewelry and watch retailers, and premium lifestyle brands sold through selective distribution, boutiques, and concession counters.

Includes: Luxury Fashion & Accessories, Fine Jewelry & Watches, Premium Beauty & Cosmetics.

 06

Retail Technology & Commerce Platforms

POS systems, e-commerce infrastructure, inventory management, order management, and omnichannel fulfillment solutions powering modern retail operations.

Includes: POS & Checkout Systems, E-commerce Platforms, Inventory & Order Management.

How to Choose a Niche

Start by defining the channel (online vs. in-store vs. omnichannel) and merchandise category before exploring technology or operations pain points. Examples: inventory optimization software for multi-location specialty retailers; returns management platforms for DTC e-commerce brands; demand forecasting tools for grocery chains managing perishable SKUs; clienteling apps for luxury boutiques tracking high-value customer relationships.
