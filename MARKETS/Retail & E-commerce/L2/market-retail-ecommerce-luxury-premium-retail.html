Luxury & Premium Retail

High-end fashion houses, fine jewelry and watch retailers, and premium beauty brands sold through selective distribution, mono-brand boutiques, department store concessions, and curated digital experiences where brand heritage and exclusivity drive pricing power.

Segments follow Bain-Altagamma luxury market definitions, Euromonitor luxury goods classifications, and McKinsey luxury retail practice categories — the three standard references for luxury market sizing and segmentation.

Sources

Bain & Company Luxury Altagamma Foundation Euromonitor Luxury Goods McKinsey Luxury

The global personal luxury goods market reached $380 billion in 2024 (Bain-Altagamma), with the top 10 conglomerates (LVMH, Kering, Richemont, Hermes) controlling 40%+ of the market. Digital channels represent 22-25% of luxury sales and growing, but 75%+ of purchases are still influenced by in-store experiences. China and the US together account for ~45% of global luxury consumption.

How to Choose a Niche

Luxury clienteling and CRM tools (Salesforce-alternative for luxury) address a market where a single VIC (Very Important Client) can generate $50K+ annual spend. Authentication technology is critical as counterfeiting reaches $500B+ globally. Digital product passports for luxury (EU regulation) and luxury resale/recommerce (The RealReal model) are high-growth niches. Avoid mass-market retail approaches — luxury operates on fundamentally different economics.
