Grocery & Convenience Retail

Supermarkets, convenience stores, and online grocery platforms selling perishable and packaged food, beverages, and household essentials with high purchase frequency, thin margins, and complex cold-chain logistics.

Segments follow NAICS 4451 (Grocery Stores) and 4453 (Convenience Retailers) classifications, FMI (Food Marketing Institute) store format categories, and Euromonitor grocery retailing channel definitions.

Sources

FMI — Food Industry Research US Census Retail Trade Euromonitor Grocery McKinsey Grocery

The global grocery market exceeds $12 trillion annually, with US grocery at ~$1.1T. Online grocery penetration reached 12% in the US (up from 3% pre-pandemic) but profitability remains elusive for most delivery models. Convenience stores generate $700B+ in US sales with 150,000+ locations, increasingly competing on fresh food and foodservice rather than just fuel and tobacco.

How to Choose a Niche

Grocery technology is a massive opportunity with low tech penetration compared to general retail. Fresh food waste reduction software targets a $400B+ global food waste problem. Online grocery profitability tools (micro-fulfillment, dark stores, delivery route optimization) are critical as every major grocer scales e-commerce. C-store modernization — mobile checkout, loyalty, and fresh food management — serves a 150K+ location market undergoing format transformation.
