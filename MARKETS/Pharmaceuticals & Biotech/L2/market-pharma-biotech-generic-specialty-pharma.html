Part A — Level 2
Generic & Specialty Pharma

Off-patent drug manufacturers producing ANDA-approved generics, plus specialty pharma companies focused on niche indications, orphan drugs, and complex formulations.

Segments follow FDA ANDA/505(b)(2) regulatory pathways, IQVIA generic dispensing data, Evaluate Pharma specialty drug forecasts, and FDA Office of Orphan Products designations.

Sources

FDA ANDA FDA Orphan Products IQVIA Institute AAM (Generic Medicines)

Generics account for 90% of US prescriptions but only 18% of drug spending — while specialty drugs represent just 2% of prescriptions but 55% of spending. This asymmetry defines the competitive dynamics of each sub-segment.

How to Choose a Niche

Complex generics (injectables, inhalation, ophthalmic, topical) have higher barriers to entry and better margins than commodity oral solids. Orphan drug development offers regulatory incentives including 7-year exclusivity and tax credits. Specialty distribution requires specialized cold chain and patient support services.
