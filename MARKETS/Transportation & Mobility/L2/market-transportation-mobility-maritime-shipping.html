Maritime Shipping

Ocean-based transport of goods and raw materials carrying 80% of global trade by volume across container, bulk, tanker, and specialized vessel segments connecting 900+ ports worldwide.

Segments derived from IMO vessel classifications, UNCTAD Review of Maritime Transport categories, Clarksons Research vessel type breakdowns, and Baltic Exchange shipping indices.

Sources

IMO UNCTAD Clarksons Baltic Exchange

Maritime shipping moves $14T+ in goods annually but remains one of the least digitized industries. Over 50% of shipping documentation is still paper-based, and the average container vessel makes 5-7 port calls per voyage — each generating 50+ documents. IMO 2023 carbon intensity regulations (CII) are forcing fleet-wide retrofits and creating urgent demand for emissions monitoring, voyage optimization, and compliance reporting solutions.

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on compliance-driven pain points: IMO CII ratings, EU ETS for shipping (launched 2024), and SOLAS container weight verification all create mandatory workflows that ship operators and ports must execute. Documentation digitization (bills of lading, customs, certificates of origin) remains a massive unsolved problem — Maersk alone processes 30M+ paper documents per year.
