Road Freight & Trucking

The backbone of domestic goods movement, covering full-load, partial-load, last-mile, and specialized hauling operations that collectively move 72.5% of all freight tonnage in the United States alone.

Segments derived from American Trucking Associations (ATA) industry classifications, FHWA Freight Analysis Framework categories, and Bureau of Transportation Statistics modal breakdowns.

Sources

ATA FHWA FAF BTS McKinsey

U.S. trucking is an $875B+ industry employing 3.5 million drivers, yet it remains one of the most fragmented sectors: 97% of carriers operate 20 or fewer trucks. This extreme fragmentation creates massive inefficiency — trucks run empty 35% of miles driven — and enormous opportunity for technology-enabled consolidation, matching, and optimization.

How to Choose a Niche

Target segments where driver shortage pain is highest (currently 78,000 unfilled positions per ATA) and where manual processes like paper BOLs, phone-based dispatch, and spreadsheet-based load planning create the most friction. Owner-operators and small carriers (1-5 trucks) remain massively underserved by existing TMS solutions.
