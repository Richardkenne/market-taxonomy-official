Urban Mobility & Transit

City-level transportation systems spanning public transit networks, ride-hailing platforms, shared micro-mobility, and for-hire vehicle services that collectively move billions of urban passengers daily across the world's metro areas.

Segments derived from American Public Transportation Association (APTA) mode classifications, UITP global transit data, McKinsey Center for Future Mobility frameworks, and FTA National Transit Database categories.

Urban mobility is being reshaped by three converging forces: electrification (EV transit buses up 40% YoY), platformization (Uber/Lyft processing 25M+ rides daily), and Mobility-as-a-Service (MaaS) integration. Yet 60% of U.S. transit agencies still rely on fixed-route scheduling designed decades ago, and fare collection systems average 15+ years old — creating massive modernization demand.

How to Choose a Niche

Public transit agencies are the highest-value B2G opportunity — they have mandatory budgets, long procurement cycles (5-10 year contracts), and acute modernization needs in fare collection, real-time passenger information, and paratransit scheduling. Micro-mobility operators face extreme unit economics pressure, making fleet operations and maintenance optimization tools critical for survival.
