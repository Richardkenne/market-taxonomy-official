Rail Transport

Freight and passenger rail networks spanning over 1 million km of track globally, moving 12 billion tonnes of freight and 35+ billion passenger-journeys annually across Class I railroads, commuter systems, and high-speed networks.

Segments derived from Association of American Railroads (AAR) classifications, Federal Railroad Administration (FRA) categories, International Union of Railways (UIC) statistics, and Surface Transportation Board (STB) commodity groupings.

Rail is the most fuel-efficient surface freight mode — moving one ton of cargo 492 miles per gallon of fuel vs. 130 miles for trucks (AAR data). Yet the U.S. freight rail network is controlled by just 7 Class I railroads generating $80B+ in revenue, while passenger rail remains chronically underfunded. The $66B Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act is driving a generational investment cycle in both freight and passenger rail modernization.

How to Choose a Niche

Short line railroads (600+ in the U.S.) are the most underserved segment — most run on paper-based operations with minimal technology. Predictive maintenance for aging track infrastructure and rail car fleet management for lessors handling 1.6M+ freight cars represent high-value, low-competition software opportunities.
