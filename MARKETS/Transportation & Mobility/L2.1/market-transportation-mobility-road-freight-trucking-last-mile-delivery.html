Part A — Level 2.1
Last-Mile Delivery

The final leg of delivery from distribution center to end consumer, accounting for 53% of total shipping cost and driven by explosive e-commerce growth and same-day delivery expectations.

Market Context

Market Size$130B+ globally, projected to reach $200B+ by 2028 at 10%+ CAGR
Key PlayersAmazon Logistics, UPS, FedEx Ground, DHL eCommerce, DoorDash, Instacart, gig delivery platforms
StructureMix of integrated carriers, DSP networks (Amazon), and gig platforms; average 1.5 delivery attempts per package
EconomicsLast-mile costs $6-10/package in urban areas, $15-25 in rural; failed deliveries cost $17.20 each on average
Last-mile delivery is the fastest-growing and most cost-intensive segment of logistics. Amazon now delivers 60%+ of its own packages through its Delivery Service Partner (DSP) network of 3,000+ small businesses, each managing 20-40 vans. The segment is seeing rapid innovation in route optimization, locker networks, and autonomous delivery vehicles, but failed deliveries and returns still cost the industry $20B+ annually.

Known Pain Points

  • Failed first-attempt deliveries waste $17+ per package — 8% of deliveries require re-attempts
  • Route optimization for 100+ stop routes with time windows remains computationally challenging for small fleets
  • Driver turnover in gig delivery exceeds 300% annually, creating constant training and quality issues
  • Proof of delivery documentation and photo capture is inconsistent across platforms
  • Reverse logistics (returns) cost 59% more than outbound delivery and lack dedicated optimization tools
