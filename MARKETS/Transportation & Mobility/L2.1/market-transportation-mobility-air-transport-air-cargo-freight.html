Part A — Level 2.1
Air Cargo & Freight

Dedicated freighter aircraft and belly-hold cargo operations moving 60+ million tonnes of high-value, time-sensitive goods annually, representing 35% of global trade by value despite carrying less than 1% by volume.

Market Context

Market Size$150B+ globally; e-commerce cross-border air freight growing 15%+ annually
Key PlayersFedEx Express, UPS Airlines, DHL Aviation, Cargolux, Atlas Air, Qatar Airways Cargo, Emirates SkyCargo
StructureSplit between integrators (FedEx/UPS/DHL — 50%+ market share) and combination/all-cargo carriers
DigitizationIATA's ONE Record standard adoption under 20%; air waybills still 40%+ paper-based globally
Air cargo booking remains one of the least digitized processes in logistics — rates are often negotiated via email chains and phone calls, with capacity allocation managed on spreadsheets. The industry generates 7.9 billion paper documents annually per IATA estimates. Cross-border e-commerce (especially from China via Shein, Temu) is overwhelming legacy customs and cargo handling processes at major airports.

Known Pain Points

  • Cargo booking processes average 10+ touchpoints across agents, airlines, and handlers — no single system of record
  • ULD (Unit Load Device) tracking across 30+ airlines sharing containers has 15% loss/misplacement rate
  • Dangerous goods acceptance checks are manual and error-prone, causing 5% of shipments to be rejected at origin
  • Customs pre-clearance data submission requires different formats for each country, with ICS2 adding new complexity in the EU
  • Revenue management for cargo is 15-20 years behind passenger RM — most airlines use static rate cards vs. dynamic pricing
