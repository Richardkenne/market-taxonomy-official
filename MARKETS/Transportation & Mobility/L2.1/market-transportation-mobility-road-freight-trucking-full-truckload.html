Part A — Level 2.1
Full Truckload (FTL)

Dedicated trailer shipments where a single shipper fills an entire 53-foot trailer for point-to-point delivery, representing the largest segment of U.S. trucking by revenue.

Market Context

Market Size$450B+ (U.S.), growing 3-4% annually driven by e-commerce and nearshoring
Key PlayersJ.B. Hunt, Schneider National, Werner Enterprises, Knight-Swift, Heartland Express
Structure800,000+ carriers; 97% operate fewer than 20 trucks; top 10 carriers hold ~10% market share
CycleHighly cyclical — rates swing 20-30% between tight and loose freight markets (2-3 year cycles)
The FTL market's extreme fragmentation means most loads are still booked via phone calls and emails. Empty miles (deadhead) average 28% industry-wide, representing $50B+ in wasted capacity annually. The ELD mandate (2017) created a data foundation, but most small carriers lack tools to use this data for route optimization or capacity planning.

Known Pain Points

  • Deadhead miles waste 28% of capacity — carriers lack tools to find backhaul loads efficiently
  • Driver turnover exceeds 90% annually at large carriers, creating constant recruiting and onboarding costs
  • Paper-based bills of lading and proof of delivery delay payment cycles by 30-45 days
  • Rate volatility makes contract pricing unpredictable — spot vs. contract spreads swing $0.50+/mile
  • Hours of Service (HOS) compliance management remains manual for most small fleets
