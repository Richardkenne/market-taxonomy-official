Part A — Level 2.1
Micro-Mobility

Shared and personal e-scooters, e-bikes, and docked bike-sharing systems for short-distance urban trips.

These sources define how micro-mobility is segmented by vehicle type, ownership model, and city regulatory framework.

Sources

McKinsey Micro-Mobility NABSA Bike Share ITDP Cycling SAE Micro-Mobility

Market Context

Size / Scale$5B+ global shared micro-mobility revenue
Growth signalE-bike sales +25% CAGR; shared scooter consolidating
Key playersLime, Tier, Voi, Lyft (bikes), Dott, Neuron
GeographyEurope leads in shared fleets; US and Asia in personal e-bike sales
Shared micro-mobility operators face unit economics challenges: average vehicle lifespan of 12-18 months, 3-5 rides per day per vehicle, and vandalism/theft rates of 15-30% annually in some markets.

Known Pain Points

  • Fleet rebalancing and charging logistics consuming 30-40% of operating costs
  • Vehicle durability and vandalism-resistant design driving high replacement costs
  • City permit compliance with geofencing, speed limits, and parking zones
  • Integration with public transit apps for seamless multimodal trip planning

How to Choose a Niche

Fleet operations optimization (rebalancing algorithms, charging logistics, maintenance scheduling) is the highest-value B2B opportunity. City regulators need permit management and compliance monitoring tools as micro-mobility regulations evolve rapidly.
