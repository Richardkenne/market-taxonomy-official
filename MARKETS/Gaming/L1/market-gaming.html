26 Gaming

Gaming markets are segmented by platform, monetization model, and audience — each with distinct development economics, distribution dynamics, and player engagement patterns.

We reference two types of sources: industry trade associations that track revenue by platform and region, and market-intelligence research used to size segments and forecast gaming spending trends. Use these to validate market definitions before narrowing to a specific platform or genre.

01

Mobile Gaming

Free-to-play and premium games for smartphones and tablets — the largest gaming segment by player count, driven by in-app purchases and ad monetization.

Includes: Casual Games, Mid-Core Games, Hypercasual, Puzzle, Strategy.

 02

PC & Console Gaming

Desktop and console gaming spanning AAA titles, indie games, and competitive multiplayer — with premium pricing, subscription services, and strong modding communities.

Includes: AAA Titles, Indie Games, Console Exclusives, PC Games, Subscription Services.

 03

Online Gambling & iGaming

Digital gambling platforms including online casinos, sports betting, poker, and lottery — driven by regulatory expansion, mobile adoption, and real-money gaming mechanics.

Includes: Online Casinos, Sports Betting, Poker, Lottery, Fantasy Sports.

 04

Esports & Competitive Gaming

Organized competitive gaming leagues, tournaments, and streaming ecosystems monetized through sponsorships, media rights, merchandise, and betting.

Includes: Professional Leagues, Tournament Platforms, Team Organizations, Gaming Streamers.

 05

Game Development & Tools

Game engines, SDKs, asset marketplaces, and backend services that power game creation — from Unity and Unreal to specialized audio, networking, and analytics tools.

Includes: Game Engines, Asset Stores, Dev SDKs, Backend-as-a-Service, QA Tools.

 06

Cloud Gaming & Emerging

Server-side game streaming, VR/AR gaming, and blockchain-based gaming platforms removing hardware barriers and exploring new interaction and ownership models.

Includes: Cloud Streaming, VR Gaming, AR Experiences, Blockchain Gaming, Metaverse Platforms.

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on segments with high creator fragmentation and monetization complexity where tooling demand is strong. Examples: analytics dashboards for indie mobile game studios optimizing in-app purchase funnels; live-ops and A/B testing infrastructure for online game operators; tournament management platforms for grassroots esports organizers.
