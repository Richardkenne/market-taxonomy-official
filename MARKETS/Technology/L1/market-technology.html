21 Technology

Technology markets are segmented by infrastructure layer, service model, and adoption stage — each with distinct capital requirements, competitive dynamics, and platform economics.

We reference two types of sources: global classification standards used by investors and index providers, and market-intelligence research used to size segments and track technology spending. Use these to validate market definitions before narrowing to a specific segment or geography.

Semiconductors & Electronic Components

Chip design, fabrication, packaging, and electronic component manufacturing powering computing, communications, and industrial systems.

Includes: Logic & Processors, Memory & Storage ICs, Foundry & Advanced Packaging, Analog & Mixed-Signal.

 02

Enterprise Software & SaaS

Business applications, productivity platforms, database and middleware infrastructure, and vertical-specific software delivered increasingly through cloud subscription models.

Includes: ERP & Business Applications, Collaboration & Productivity, Database & Middleware, Vertical Industry Software.

 03

Cloud Computing & Infrastructure

Public cloud platforms, data centers, edge computing, and infrastructure-as-a-service enabling on-demand compute, storage, and networking.

Includes: Public Cloud & IaaS, Data Centers & Colocation, Edge Computing & CDN.

 04

IT Services & Consulting

Systems integration, managed services, IT outsourcing, and technology consulting helping enterprises plan, build, and run IT environments.

Includes: Systems Integration, Managed IT Services, IT Outsourcing & BPO.

 05

Cybersecurity

Network security, endpoint protection, identity management, and threat intelligence services defending organizations against digital threats.

Includes: Network & Endpoint Security, Identity & Access Management, Cloud Security & SASE.

 06

Artificial Intelligence & Data

Machine learning infrastructure, data engineering platforms, big data analytics, and AI model deployment serving enterprise and research use cases.

Includes: ML Platforms & MLOps, Data Engineering & Analytics, Generative AI & Foundation Models.

 07

Emerging Technologies

Quantum computing, blockchain, extended reality (AR/VR), and other frontier technologies moving from research to early commercial deployment.

Includes: Quantum Computing, Blockchain & Web3, Extended Reality (AR/VR/MR).

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on segments with rapid technology cycles, high operational complexity, or emerging compliance mandates. Examples: cloud cost optimization tools for multi-cloud enterprises; automated penetration testing for mid-market companies without security teams; semiconductor supply chain visibility platforms for electronics manufacturers managing chip shortages.
