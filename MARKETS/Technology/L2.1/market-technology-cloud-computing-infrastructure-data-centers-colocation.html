Market Context

Size / Scale~$120B+ market; hyperscale data center buildout accelerating with $200B+ annual capex from top 4 hyperscalers combined
Growth signalStrong — AI training clusters require massive power (100MW+ per facility); data center construction constrained by power availability
Key playersEquinix, Digital Realty, CyrusOne, QTS, NTT, Vantage; Hyperscale: AWS, Microsoft, Google, Meta building their own
GeographyUS (Virginia, Texas, Oregon dominant); Europe (Amsterdam, Frankfurt, London, Dublin); Asia (Singapore, Tokyo, Mumbai); Middle East emerging
Power availability is the new bottleneck — data centers now consume 4-5% of US electricity and AI is projected to double that by 2030. Sustainable cooling and nuclear power for data centers are the next frontiers.

Known Pain Points

  • Power procurement for new data centers takes 3-5 years — no effective marketplace or optimization tools exist
  • Data center cooling accounts for 30-40% of energy costs — liquid cooling adoption is ad hoc with no standard design tools
  • Physical infrastructure management (DCIM) tools outdated — most facilities still managed with spreadsheets and manual walkthroughs
  • Capacity planning across multiple colocation facilities lacks real-time visibility into power, space, and network utilization

How to Choose a Niche

Data center infrastructure management (DCIM) software is ripe for modernization — the installed base runs on 15-year-old tools. Power and cooling optimization using ML has proven ROI. Sustainability reporting and carbon tracking for data centers is a regulatory-driven opportunity. Avoid the construction side — focus on operational software.
