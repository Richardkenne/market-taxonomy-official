Generative AI & Foundation Models

Market Context

Size / Scale~$65B+ market (2024); projected to reach $200B+ by 2028 at 80%+ CAGR — fastest-growing technology category in history
Growth signalVery strong — every enterprise is evaluating or deploying GenAI; regulatory frameworks (EU AI Act) creating compliance requirements
Key playersOpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Meta AI, Mistral, Cohere, Stability AI; Infra: NVIDIA, Hugging Face, LangChain, LlamaIndex
GeographyUS dominates (OpenAI, Anthropic, Meta); Europe strong in open-source (Mistral); China developing domestic models (Baidu, Alibaba, ByteDance)
The GenAI market is bifurcating: foundation model providers require billions in capital and will consolidate to 5-10 players, while the application layer (using models to solve specific problems) is wide open for startups with domain expertise.

Known Pain Points

  • Enterprise GenAI adoption blocked by hallucination risk — no reliable way to guarantee factual accuracy for critical workflows
  • LLM evaluation and testing frameworks immature — organizations cannot systematically measure quality, safety, and performance
  • RAG (retrieval-augmented generation) implementation is complex — chunking strategies, embedding quality, and retrieval relevance poorly understood
  • AI cost management unpredictable — token-based pricing makes it difficult to forecast GenAI spending at enterprise scale

How to Choose a Niche

The GenAI application layer has the best risk/reward: vertical AI agents for specific industries (legal document review, medical coding, financial analysis) show strongest PMF. LLM evaluation and testing frameworks are critical infrastructure with no dominant player. RAG-as-a-service and knowledge management platforms leverage GenAI for high-value enterprise use cases. AI safety and compliance tools (bias detection, content filtering, audit trails) have regulatory tailwinds from the EU AI Act.
