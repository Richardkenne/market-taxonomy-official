Capital Markets

Sales and trading of equities, fixed income, currencies, and commodities (FICC) — including market-making, prime brokerage, securities financing, and electronic trading.

Market Context

Size / Scale~$30B annual S&T revenue at top 12 banks; $130T+ daily global trading volume
Growth signalElectronic trading now 80%+ of equities volume; FICC electronification accelerating
Key playersJPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, Citadel Securities, Jane Street, Virtu
GeographyUS/UK (dominant), Hong Kong, Tokyo, Frankfurt — regulatory fragmentation by jurisdiction
Capital markets is increasingly a technology arms race. Firms investing in low-latency infrastructure, alternative data, and AI-driven execution algorithms are taking share from traditional voice-brokered operations.

Known Pain Points

  • Fixed income and credit markets remain 40%+ voice-brokered — electronification lagging equities by a decade
  • Post-trade reconciliation and settlement (T+1 transition) creating integration challenges across custodians
  • Market surveillance and trade compliance monitoring generating excessive false positives (95%+)
  • Multi-asset portfolio risk analytics fragmented across systems — no unified real-time view

How to Choose a Niche

Fixed income electronification, post-trade settlement automation, and trade surveillance false-positive reduction are the highest-value niches. Target mid-tier broker-dealers and regional banks upgrading their trading infrastructure.
