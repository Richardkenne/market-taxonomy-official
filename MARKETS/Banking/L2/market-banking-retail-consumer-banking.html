Retail & Consumer Banking

Mass-market deposit accounts, consumer lending, credit cards, and personal financial services delivered through branch networks and digital channels to individual consumers and households.

Segments follow FDIC call report categories, Federal Reserve consumer credit data classifications, and NAICS 522110-522130 subdivisions for depository credit intermediation.

Sources

FDIC Quarterly Banking Profile Fed G.19 Consumer Credit McKinsey Retail Banking S&P Global Market Intel

Global retail banking revenues exceed $2.3 trillion annually (McKinsey Global Banking Review). Deposits remain the largest revenue pool but net interest margin compression and digital migration are shifting value toward fee-based products — cards, wealth, and digital payments now represent 40%+ of retail banking profits at leading institutions.

How to Choose a Niche

Digital account opening, personal financial management tools, and credit decisioning automation are high-value software opportunities. Target mid-tier banks ($1B-$50B assets) struggling to match neobank UX while maintaining compliance. Deposit pricing optimization and customer churn prediction tools have strong PMF signals.
