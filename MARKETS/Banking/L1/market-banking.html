Part A — Level 1
17 Banking

Banking markets are segmented by customer type, product category, and delivery model — each with distinct regulatory capital requirements, risk profiles, and digital transformation dynamics.

We reference two types of sources: global classification standards used by banking regulators and central banks, and market-intelligence research used to size segments and track lending, deposit, and fintech trends. Use these to validate market definitions before narrowing to a specific banking segment or geography.

How market categories are recognized How TAM is calculated

These categories follow NAICS 522 divisions and GICS Banks sub-industry classifications — segmenting by customer segment (retail, commercial, mortgage), institution type (community, digital), and delivery model.

01

Retail & Consumer Banking

Mass-market deposit accounts, consumer lending, credit cards, and personal financial services delivered through branch networks and digital channels.

Includes: Checking & Savings, Credit Cards, Personal Loans, Auto Lending, Branch Banking.

 02

Commercial & Business Banking

Lending, treasury management, and deposit services for small businesses, mid-market companies, and large corporations — with relationship-driven pricing.

Includes: SME Lending, Commercial Real Estate, Treasury Management, Trade Finance, Cash Management.

 03

Mortgage Banking

Origination, underwriting, servicing, and secondary-market trading of residential and commercial mortgage loans — highly rate-sensitive with complex compliance requirements.

Includes: Mortgage Origination, Loan Servicing, Secondary Markets, Title & Escrow, Warehouse Lending.

 04

Community & Credit Union Banking

Community banks and credit unions serving local markets with relationship-based lending, member services, and shared-branch networks — often with cooperative governance.

Includes: Community Banks, Credit Unions, CDFIs, Mutual Savings Banks, Shared Branching.

 05

Digital & Challenger Banking

Branchless neobanks, banking-as-a-service platforms, and embedded finance providers delivering accounts, cards, and lending through API-first architectures.

Includes: Neobanks, BaaS Platforms, Embedded Banking, Mobile-First Banks, Open Banking APIs.

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on segments with high compliance burden and fragmented operators. Examples: BSA/AML transaction monitoring automation for community banks under $10B in assets; digital account opening workflows for credit unions; loan origination document automation for mid-tier mortgage lenders.
