Capital Markets & Investment Banking

Securities trading, underwriting, M&A advisory, and institutional brokerage connecting issuers with investors across equity, debt, and derivatives markets — generating ~$600B in annual revenue globally.

Segments follow BIS capital market statistics, BCG Global Capital Markets report classifications, and Dealogic/Refinitiv league table categories aligned with GICS Diversified Financials sub-industries.

Sources

BIS Securities Stats BCG Capital Markets McKinsey FS Insights IMF Financial Stability

Global capital markets revenue reached ~$600B in 2024 (BCG). Electronic trading now accounts for 80%+ of equity volume and 50%+ of fixed income. The shift toward passive investing, private markets, and AI-driven trading is compressing traditional advisory fees while creating new technology-driven revenue pools.

How to Choose a Niche

Capital markets technology is highly specialized. The best entry points are trade surveillance and compliance tools (MiFID II, Dodd-Frank), fixed-income electronic trading platforms, and data/analytics infrastructure. Target tier-2 broker-dealers and regional exchanges — they have budget but lack the in-house tech teams of Goldman or JPMorgan.
