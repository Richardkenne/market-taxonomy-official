Institutional Asset Management

Mutual funds, ETFs, index funds, and institutional mandates managed for pension funds, sovereign wealth funds, endowments, and insurance companies — dominated by scale, fee compression, and passive investing.

Data sourced from BIS, IMF, McKinsey, Deloitte, BCG, and World Bank financial services research databases.

Sources

BCG Asset Mgmt McKinsey Asset Mgmt IMF Financial Stability Investment Company Institute

Market Context

Size / Scale~$280B revenue; $70T+ in institutional AUM globally; mutual funds and ETFs at $65T
Growth signalRevenue under pressure — passive funds growing at 15%+ while active management fees declining 3-5% annually
Key playersBlackRock, Vanguard, Fidelity, State Street, Amundi, PIMCO, Capital Group, T. Rowe Price
GeographyUS (55% of global fund AUM), Europe (25%), Asia-Pacific (15%), with Middle East SWFs growing
The active-to-passive shift has moved $5T+ in AUM over the past decade, but active management still commands higher fees. The next wave of disruption is direct indexing and personalized portfolios at scale, blurring the line between active and passive.

Known Pain Points

  • Fee compression squeezing margins — average equity fund fee down from 80bps to 40bps in a decade
  • Fund distribution relies on intermediary platforms with complex fee-sharing arrangements
  • ESG data integration and reporting requirements adding operational complexity
  • Portfolio risk management and compliance monitoring across multiple mandates fragmented

How to Choose a Niche

Investment operations automation (trade reconciliation, NAV calculation, regulatory reporting) is the strongest niche — fund companies spend 30-40% of revenue on operations. Direct indexing platforms enabling mass customization of portfolios are also growing rapidly. Target mid-tier asset managers ($50-500B AUM) — they need vendor solutions but have budget to pay.
