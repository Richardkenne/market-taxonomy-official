Digital Banking

Neobanks, digital-only banks, mobile banking platforms, and online banking services — the fastest-growing retail banking segment, serving 400M+ customers globally without traditional branch networks.

Data sourced from BIS, IMF, McKinsey, Deloitte, BCG, and World Bank financial services research databases.

Sources

McKinsey Digital Banking BCG Digital Banking CB Insights Neobanks World Bank Findex

Market Context

Size / Scale~$380B revenue; 400M+ neobank customers globally
Growth signal15-20% annual revenue growth; customer acquisition 5-10x cheaper than traditional banks; profitability improving
Key playersNubank, Revolut, Monzo, N26, Chime, Tinkoff, KakaoBank, WeBank
GeographyBrazil (Nubank: 90M+), UK (Revolut: 40M+), US (Chime: 20M+), SE Asia, Africa
Neobanks reached profitability inflection in 2024 — Nubank and Revolut now profitable. The model is proven but differentiation is narrowing, pushing neobanks toward lending, investing, and B2B services for revenue diversification.

Known Pain Points

  • Customer acquisition costs rising as neobank market saturates in developed economies
  • Regulatory licensing requirements (banking charters) create multi-year barriers to entry
  • Unit economics depend on cross-selling beyond free checking — conversion rates below 10%
  • Fraud and identity verification at scale without physical presence remains challenging

How to Choose a Niche

Building a neobank is capital-intensive and crowded. Instead, sell to neobanks and digital banks — they need fraud prevention, customer engagement analytics, financial product personalization engines, and compliance automation. The picks-and-shovels play is far more profitable than being a bank.
