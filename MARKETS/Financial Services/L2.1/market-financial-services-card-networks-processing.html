Card Networks & Processing

Global card networks, payment processors, issuer processors, and acquirer platforms handling authorization, clearing, and settlement for hundreds of billions of card transactions annually.

Data sourced from BIS, IMF, McKinsey, Deloitte, BCG, and World Bank financial services research databases.

Sources

BIS CPMI Stats McKinsey Payments BCG Payments Nilson Report

Market Context

Size / Scale~$850B revenue; $40T+ in annual card transaction volume; 500B+ transactions annually
Growth signal6-8% annual growth driven by cash displacement; contactless adoption accelerating in all markets
Key playersVisa, Mastercard, UnionPay, Fiserv, FIS, Global Payments, Worldline, Adyen
GeographyUS ($10T card volume), Europe ($4T), China (UnionPay: $15T), with India and Africa fastest growing
Card networks are the most profitable business model in financial services — Visa and Mastercard earn 70%+ operating margins on a network effect that grows with every merchant and cardholder added globally.

Known Pain Points

  • Interchange regulation (Durbin, IFR) compressing issuer and acquirer margins
  • Card-not-present fraud rates 5-7x higher than in-person — costing $30B+ annually
  • Multi-acquirer management and payment routing optimization lack standardization
  • PCI DSS compliance costs $1-5M annually for mid-market merchants

How to Choose a Niche

Payment orchestration platforms that route transactions across multiple acquirers and optimize for cost/approval rates are the strongest niche. Target enterprise merchants processing $100M+ annually — they save 10-30 bps per transaction through intelligent routing.
