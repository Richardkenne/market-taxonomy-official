Revenue Cycle Management

Patient registration, insurance verification, medical coding, charge capture, billing, collections, denial management, and financial reporting across the entire provider revenue cycle.

Data sourced from HFMA revenue cycle benchmarks, CMS billing data, and KLAS RCM vendor performance reports.

Market Context

Size / Scale~$140B market (outsourced + technology); average hospital loses 3-5% of net revenue to billing errors
Growth signalStrong — AI-powered coding and denial management growing 20%+; outsourced RCM growing 10%
Key playersOptum (Change Healthcare), R1 RCM, Waystar, Athenahealth, eClinicalWorks
GeographyUS-dominant due to complexity of multi-payer system; simpler in single-payer markets
Revenue cycle is the most active AI adoption area in healthcare operations. The 2024 Change Healthcare breach exposed systemic single-point-of-failure risks, creating demand for resilient, multi-vendor RCM architectures. Denial rates have increased 20%+ since 2020, making denial prevention and management critical.

Known Pain Points

  • Claim denial rates averaging 10-15% with appeal success highly dependent on manual effort
  • Medical coding accuracy and compliance — ICD-10 has 72,000+ codes with annual updates
  • Patient financial responsibility growing (high-deductible plans) but collection rates below 50%
  • Eligibility verification and benefits checking still requiring manual payer portal lookups

How to Choose a Niche

Denial management and prevention is the highest-ROI entry point — providers lose billions annually to preventable denials. AI-assisted medical coding for specific specialties (orthopedics, cardiology, dermatology) has proven PMF. Patient payment estimation and collection tools are growing with consumerization trends. Pick a provider segment (ASCs, behavioral health, home health) rather than trying to serve all.
