Health Insurance & Managed Care

Commercial health plans, government programs (Medicare/Medicaid), and managed care organizations that finance, administer, and coordinate healthcare benefits for covered populations.

Segments follow CMS enrollment data categories, NAIC health insurance classifications, and McKinsey payer ecosystem frameworks spanning commercial, government, and managed care market structures.

US health insurance premiums exceed $1.3 trillion annually. Medicare Advantage enrollment surpassed 50% of eligible beneficiaries in 2024, fundamentally reshaping the payer landscape. Value-based care models now cover 40%+ of healthcare payments, shifting risk from payers to providers and creating new technology needs on both sides.

How to Choose a Niche

Payer technology has high barriers but massive contract values. The best entry points are tools serving self-funded employers (benefits administration, cost transparency), Medicare Advantage plan analytics, or ACO performance management platforms. Avoid competing on core claims processing — focus on analytics, member engagement, and risk adjustment layers.
