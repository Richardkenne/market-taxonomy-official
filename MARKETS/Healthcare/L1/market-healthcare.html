Part A — Level 1
20 Healthcare

Healthcare markets are segmented by care setting, service type, and operational model — each with distinct regulatory requirements, reimbursement dynamics, and technology adoption patterns.

We reference two types of sources: global classification standards used by health institutions and payers, and market-intelligence research used to size segments and track care trends. Use these to validate market definitions before narrowing to a specific care setting or geography.

These categories reflect how healthcare is organized by care function, reimbursement model, and operational context — matching NAICS 62 divisions, CMS provider classifications, and WHO service delivery frameworks.

01

Healthcare Providers

Hospitals, ambulatory surgery centers, and integrated health systems delivering inpatient and outpatient clinical care.

Includes: Acute Care Hospitals, Ambulatory Surgery Centers, Health Systems & IDNs.

 02

Care Delivery

Primary care, specialty care, behavioral health, and home-based care organized by patient journey and care setting.

Includes: Primary Care, Specialty Care, Home Health & Hospice, Behavioral Health.

 03

Diagnostics

Clinical laboratory testing, medical imaging, and pathology services that inform diagnosis and treatment decisions.

Includes: Clinical Laboratory, Medical Imaging, Pathology & Genomics.

 04

Health Services & Administration

Revenue cycle management, medical billing, credentialing, and operational services supporting clinical workflows.

Includes: Revenue Cycle Management, Claims & Payer Operations, Care Coordination.

 05

Healthcare Technology

EHR/EMR systems, telemedicine platforms, clinical decision support, and health data analytics tools.

Includes: EHR & Clinical Systems, Telehealth & Virtual Care, Health Data Analytics.

 06

Health Insurance & Managed Care

Commercial health plans, government programs (Medicare/Medicaid), and managed care organizations that finance and coordinate care delivery.

Includes: Commercial Health Insurance, Government Programs, Managed Care Organizations.

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on segments with recurring operational friction and a clear reimbursement or budget owner. Examples: prior authorization automation for specialty clinics; patient intake software for independent primary care practices; clinical documentation tools for home health agencies.
