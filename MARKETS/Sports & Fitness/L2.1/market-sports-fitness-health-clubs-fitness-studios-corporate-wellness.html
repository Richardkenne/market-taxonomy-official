Corporate Wellness Programs

Employer-sponsored fitness benefits, on-site facilities, wellness stipends, and health program partnerships targeting employee productivity, healthcare cost reduction, and talent retention.

Data from Wellhub market research, SHRM employee benefits surveys, RAND Health wellness program studies, and Mercer health benefits analysis.

Sources

WellhubSHRMRAND HealthMercer

Market Context

Size / Scale~$12B market
Growth signalGrowing 7% annually; mental health and holistic wellness expanding scope
Key playersVirgin Pulse, Wellhub (formerly Gympass), ClassPass Corporate, Peloton Corporate, Headspace for Work
GeographyNorth America (50%), Europe (30%), Asia Pacific (15%)
Corporate wellness ROI averages $3.27 saved for every $1 spent on wellness programs. 83% of large employers offer wellness programs. The shift to remote/hybrid work expanded demand for digital-first wellness platforms that work anywhere.

Known Pain Points

  • Measuring and proving wellness program ROI to CFOs
  • Employee engagement rates averaging only 20-40% of eligible employees
  • Integration with health insurance and benefits administration systems
  • Personalizing wellness offerings across diverse, distributed workforces

How to Choose a Niche

ROI measurement and reporting tools for HR buyers are a key differentiator. Wellness benefit aggregation platforms (one card for gym, mental health, nutrition) reduce admin burden. Remote-first wellness solutions have a larger addressable market than on-site only.
