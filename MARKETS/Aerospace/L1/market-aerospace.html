39 Aerospace

Aerospace markets are segmented by vehicle type, mission profile, and value chain position — each with distinct certification requirements, procurement cycles, and technology readiness levels.

We reference two types of sources: global classification standards used by aviation regulators and defense agencies, and market-intelligence research used to size segments and track fleet and order trends. Use these to validate market definitions before narrowing to a specific platform or geography.

These categories follow NAICS 3364 and GICS Aerospace & Defense sub-industry groupings — segmenting by platform type (commercial, military, space) and value chain role (OEM, MRO, components, new mobility).

01

Commercial Aviation OEM & MRO

Design, manufacture, and lifecycle maintenance of commercial aircraft — including narrowbody and widebody programs, engine overhaul, and airframe heavy checks.

Includes: Aircraft OEMs, Engine MRO, Airframe Maintenance, Component Repair, Line Maintenance.

 02

Military & Defense Aviation

Fighter, transport, and rotorcraft platforms procured through government defense budgets — including weapons integration, mission systems, and sustainment contracts.

Includes: Combat Aircraft, Military Rotorcraft, Tankers & Transports, Mission Systems, Defense Sustainment.

 03

Space Launch & Satellites

Launch vehicle manufacturing and operations, satellite constellation deployment, and commercial space services including rideshare and hosted payloads.

Includes: Launch Vehicles, Communication Satellites, Earth Observation, Navigation Constellations, Ground Systems.

 04

Space Exploration & Science

Deep-space missions, planetary science, space station operations, and in-space manufacturing — primarily government-funded with growing commercial participation.

Includes: Lunar & Mars Programs, Space Station Services, Scientific Instruments, In-Space Manufacturing.

 05

Aerospace Components & Supply Chain

Tier 1-3 suppliers of avionics, propulsion systems, aerostructures, and landing gear — with AS9100 quality standards and long qualification cycles.

Includes: Avionics & Electronics, Propulsion & Engines, Aerostructures, Landing Gear, Raw Materials.

 06

Advanced Air Mobility & UAV

Electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft, commercial drones, and urban air mobility platforms — an emerging segment with evolving certification paths.

Includes: eVTOL Aircraft, Commercial Drones, Counter-UAS, UTM Systems, Drone Delivery.

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on segments with long contract cycles, strict compliance requirements, and manual-intensive workflows. Examples: digital work-order management for MRO shops; AS9100 audit preparation software for Tier 2 suppliers; flight-data analytics dashboards for regional airline operations teams.
