Market Context

Size / Scale~$25B market
Growth signalCalifornia mandating 10% recycled water by 2035; Singapore targets 85% reuse by 2060
Key playersXylem, Veolia, SUEZ, Fluence, Pureflow
GeographyUS (30%), MENA (20%), Asia Pacific (25%), Europe (15%)
Less than 1% of municipal wastewater is currently reused globally. Direct potable reuse (DPR) projects are expanding in Texas, California, and Singapore. Agricultural water reuse could reduce freshwater irrigation demand by 15-20%. Public perception ("toilet to tap") remains the biggest non-technical barrier.

Known Pain Points

  • Public perception barriers for potable reuse despite proven safety
  • Micropollutant and pharmaceutical residue removal requires advanced treatment adding $1-3/m3
  • Regulatory frameworks for DPR are inconsistent — only a few states have clear standards
  • Agricultural reuse requires balancing salinity and nutrient levels for crop compatibility

How to Choose a Niche

Direct potable reuse technology serves the most water-stressed regions with the strongest mandates. Agricultural recycled water serves the largest volume market. Building-scale greywater recycling is growing in green building codes.
