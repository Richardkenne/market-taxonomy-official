02 Construction & Infrastructure

Construction & Infrastructure markets are segmented by project type, delivery method, and professional discipline — each with distinct procurement models, permitting requirements, and labor dynamics.

We reference two types of sources: global classification standards used by construction industry associations and government agencies, and market-intelligence research used to size segments and track construction starts, backlog, and spending trends. Use these to validate market definitions before narrowing to a specific project type or geography.

01

Residential Construction

Single-family homebuilding, multi-family development, and residential renovation — driven by housing starts, mortgage rates, and local permitting.

Includes: Single-Family Homebuilding, Multi-Family Development, Residential Renovation, Custom Homes.

 02

Commercial Construction

Office towers, retail centers, hospitals, schools, and mixed-use projects — with design-build, CM-at-risk, and integrated project delivery methods.

Includes: Office Buildings, Retail & Hospitality, Healthcare Facilities, Educational Institutions, Data Centers.

 03

Civil Infrastructure

Roads, bridges, rail systems, water treatment plants, and utility networks — primarily publicly funded with long procurement cycles and prevailing wage requirements.

Includes: Roads & Highways, Bridges & Tunnels, Rail & Transit, Water & Wastewater, Utility Infrastructure.

 04

Engineering & Design

Architectural design, structural engineering, MEP engineering, and environmental consulting services supporting pre-construction and design phases.

Includes: Architecture Firms, Civil Engineering, MEP Engineering, Environmental Consulting, Surveying.

 05

Construction Services

General contracting, specialty trade contractors, project management, and site services — the execution layer coordinating labor, materials, and subcontractors.

Includes: General Contractors, Specialty Trades, Project Management, Site Preparation, Safety & Compliance.

 06

Construction Technology

BIM platforms, project scheduling software, drone-based site monitoring, and modular construction systems digitizing the built environment.

Includes: BIM & 3D Modeling, Scheduling & PM Software, Site Monitoring & Drones, Modular & Prefab, Safety Tech.

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on segments with coordination-heavy workflows and clear cost leakage points. Examples: subcontractor bid management software for mid-size general contractors; daily report and safety compliance apps for specialty trade crews; BIM clash detection tools for MEP engineering firms.
