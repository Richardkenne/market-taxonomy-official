Oil & Gas (Upstream)

Exploration, development, and production of crude oil and natural gas reserves — the foundation of the global hydrocarbon supply chain spanning onshore and offshore basins worldwide.

Segments are derived from S&P Global Commodity Insights upstream classifications, Rystad Energy activity breakdowns, and IEA World Energy Investment categories covering exploration, production, oilfield services, and drilling operations.

Sources

S&P Global Commodity Insights Rystad Energy IEA World Energy Investment Wood Mackenzie Upstream

Global upstream capital expenditure reached ~$580 billion in 2024 according to the IEA, yet the industry faces persistent challenges in workforce shortages, ESG compliance, and digital transformation of legacy operations — creating opportunities for specialized technology and services providers.

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on recurring operational pain points rather than one-time capital projects. The upstream sector rewards solutions that reduce well costs, improve recovery rates, or accelerate time-to-production. Consider whether your target customers are IOCs (large, slow procurement) or independents (faster decisions, tighter budgets). Digital solutions for reservoir monitoring, predictive maintenance, and emissions tracking are growing fastest.
