Oil & Gas (Midstream & Downstream)

Transportation, processing, refining, and distribution of petroleum products and natural gas — connecting wellheads to end consumers through pipelines, refineries, and retail networks.

Segments follow EIA petroleum supply chain classifications, S&P Global midstream/downstream sector definitions, and IEA refining and gas processing categories used across industry analysis.

Sources

EIA Petroleum Data S&P Global Commodity Insights IEA Oil Analysis McKinsey Oil & Gas

Global refining capacity stands at ~103 million barrels/day with significant overcapacity in some regions and deficit in others. The midstream sector faces a $1.3 trillion pipeline infrastructure gap while downstream players navigate the energy transition by investing in biofuels and petrochemical integration.

How to Choose a Niche

Midstream and downstream are capital-intensive with long asset lives, so look for software, compliance, and optimization niches rather than competing on infrastructure. Pipeline integrity monitoring, refinery yield optimization, and fuel logistics digitization are underserved areas. Consider regulatory-driven opportunities — emissions monitoring, fuel quality compliance, and pipeline safety mandates create recurring demand.
