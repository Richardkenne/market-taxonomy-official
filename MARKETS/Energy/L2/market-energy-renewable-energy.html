Renewable Energy

Power generation from naturally replenishing sources — solar, wind, hydro, biomass, and geothermal — driving the global energy transition with over $700 billion invested annually.

Segments follow IRENA renewable energy classifications, BloombergNEF technology categories, and IEA Renewables report breakdowns covering all major clean energy generation technologies.

Sources

IRENA Publications BloombergNEF IEA Renewables 2024 Wood Mackenzie Power

Renewable energy capacity additions hit a record 510 GW in 2023 according to IRENA, with solar PV alone accounting for 75% of new additions. The sector's rapid growth creates massive demand for grid integration, supply chain management, and project development services.

How to Choose a Niche

Renewable energy is growing so fast that bottlenecks constantly shift — from permitting delays to grid interconnection queues to supply chain constraints. Focus on solving friction points rather than competing on hardware. Software for site assessment, project financing platforms, O&M optimization, and grid compliance tools serve recurring needs. Consider the maturity curve: solar and onshore wind are commoditized (margins in services), while offshore wind and geothermal have higher barriers but less competition.
