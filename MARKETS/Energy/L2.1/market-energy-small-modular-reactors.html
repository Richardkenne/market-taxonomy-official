Part A — Level 2.1
Small Modular Reactors (SMRs)

Factory-built reactors under 300 MWe designed for modular deployment including light-water SMRs, molten salt, high-temperature gas, and sodium-cooled fast reactors targeting industrial, remote, and data center applications.

Data sourced from IAEA SMR technology database, World Nuclear Association SMR pages, U.S. DOE Advanced Reactor Demonstration Program, and Third Way Nuclear Innovation tracker.

Market Context

Size / Scale~$10B committed pipeline; 80+ SMR designs globally; first commercial units expected 2028-2030
Growth signalStrong — Microsoft, Google, Amazon signing nuclear PPAs; $30B+ in corporate/government commitments
Key playersX-energy, Kairos Power, TerraPower, NuScale, GE Hitachi, Rolls-Royce SMR, Holtec
GeographyUS (most designs), Canada (leading licensing), UK, China, South Korea, Romania
SMRs are the most investable nuclear technology with strong tech company backing, but no Western design has reached commercial operation yet — creating high-risk, high-reward dynamics.

Known Pain Points

  • Licensing timelines uncertain — NRC design certification takes 3-5+ years per design
  • First-of-a-kind (FOAK) cost estimates unreliable — NuScale cost per MWh doubled before cancellation
  • Supply chain for advanced reactor components (HALEU fuel, specialty materials) immature
  • Siting and permitting for SMRs at industrial/data center sites unprecedented

How to Choose a Niche

SMR supply chain, HALEU fuel logistics, and nuclear licensing software are emerging niches. Target the ecosystem around SMR developers rather than competing in reactor design. Simulation and training tools for new reactor types are also underserved.
