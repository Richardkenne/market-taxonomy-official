Hydrogen Energy Storage

Green, blue, and grey hydrogen production via electrolysis and SMR, hydrogen storage in salt caverns and tanks, fuel cell systems, and hydrogen pipeline infrastructure for long-duration energy storage.

Data sourced from Hydrogen Council reports, IEA Global Hydrogen Review, BloombergNEF Hydrogen Economy Outlook, and IRENA green hydrogen cost analysis.

Sources

Hydrogen CouncilIEA Hydrogen ReviewBloombergNEF H2IRENA Green H2

Market Context

Size / Scale~$25B current market; 95M tonnes/yr production (mostly grey); green hydrogen <1% but scaling
Growth signalStrong policy support — $280B+ in government subsidies announced globally; green H2 costs falling 50% by 2030
Key playersAir Liquide, Linde, Nel Hydrogen, ITM Power, Plug Power, Cummins, Siemens Energy
GeographyEurope (strongest mandates), US (IRA 45V credits), Middle East, Australia, Chile
Hydrogen is the most capital-intensive energy transition segment with massive government support but early-stage commercial deployment — creating a wide gap between announced projects and FID.

Known Pain Points

  • Electrolyzer utilization rates low — intermittent renewable supply creates poor economics
  • Green hydrogen certification and guarantee-of-origin systems immature and fragmented
  • Hydrogen transport and storage infrastructure nearly nonexistent outside industrial clusters
  • Project economics highly sensitive to electricity prices, subsidy structures, and offtake agreements

How to Choose a Niche

Hydrogen is early-stage — the best near-term niches are certification/tracking platforms, electrolyzer optimization software, and project finance modeling tools. Avoid hardware competition with established gas companies.
