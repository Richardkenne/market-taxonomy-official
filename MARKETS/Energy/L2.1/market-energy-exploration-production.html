Exploration & Production (E&P)

The core upstream activity — finding and extracting hydrocarbons through seismic surveying, exploratory drilling, development wells, and production operations across onshore and offshore basins.

Data sourced from Rystad Energy upstream databases, IEA World Energy Investment reports, S&P Global Commodity Insights E&P analysis, and Wood Mackenzie upstream research covering global exploration and production activity.

GeographyMiddle East (largest reserves), Permian Basin (US shale), Guyana, Brazil pre-salt, North Sea, West Africa deepwater
E&P operators face a dual challenge: maintaining production to meet current demand while managing the long-term energy transition. The biggest opportunities lie in technologies that reduce finding and development costs, improve recovery rates from mature fields, and minimize the environmental footprint of extraction operations.

Known Pain Points

  • Declining discovery rates despite increased seismic spending — new basin exploration success rates below 20%
  • Aging workforce with 50% of petroleum engineers nearing retirement and insufficient new talent pipeline
  • Methane emissions monitoring and reporting mandates creating compliance burdens across operations
  • Long project cycle times (5-10 years from discovery to first oil) increasing capital risk exposure

How to Choose a Niche

Target E&P operators' recurring operational needs rather than one-time project services. Software for production optimization, emissions monitoring, and reservoir simulation has strong recurring revenue potential. Independent E&P companies (mid-caps with $1-10B revenue) are the best initial customers — large enough to pay but small enough to make fast procurement decisions.
