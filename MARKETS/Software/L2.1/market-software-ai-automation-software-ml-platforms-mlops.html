GeographyNorth America (50%), Europe (22%), Asia-Pacific (20%)
Only 54% of ML models make it to production (Gartner, 2024) — the gap between ML research and production deployment remains the central challenge. LLM adoption is adding new complexity around prompt engineering, evaluation, fine-tuning, and cost optimization that traditional MLOps tools were not designed for.

Known Pain Points

  • LLM evaluation and testing lacks standardized frameworks — teams cannot reliably measure model quality before deployment
  • ML model monitoring in production is fragmented — data drift, model degradation, and performance regression go undetected
  • GPU compute costs for training and inference are prohibitive — optimization and cost management tools are immature
  • Feature engineering and data pipeline management consumes 60-80% of ML engineering time — feature stores remain complex to operate

How to Choose a Niche

LLM evaluation and testing frameworks, GPU inference optimization, and AI-specific observability (monitoring LLM outputs for quality, safety, and cost) are the hottest niches. Fine-tuning-as-a-service and AI gateway/router platforms are emerging categories.
