32 Security & Defense

Security and defense markets are segmented by threat domain, customer type, and capability layer — each with distinct procurement cycles, classification requirements, and regulatory frameworks.

We reference two types of sources: defense spending databases and classification standards from SIPRI, NATO, and government procurement systems, and defense industry research used to size segments and track capability investments. Use these to validate market definitions before narrowing to a specific domain or end-customer.

How market categories are recognized How TAM is calculated

01

Defense Systems & Platforms

Military hardware including combat aircraft, naval vessels, armored vehicles, missile systems, and space assets procured by national armed forces through major defense programs.

Includes: Combat Aircraft, Naval Systems, Armored Vehicles, Missile Defense, Space Systems.

 02

Cybersecurity

Network defense, endpoint protection, threat intelligence, and offensive cyber capabilities protecting government, military, and critical infrastructure from digital threats.

Includes: Network Security, Endpoint Protection, Threat Intelligence, SIEM/SOAR, Offensive Cyber.

 03

Intelligence & Surveillance

Satellite imagery, signals intelligence, UAV sensor payloads, and geospatial analytics platforms providing situational awareness for military and intelligence operations.

Includes: SIGINT/ELINT, GEOINT/Satellite Imagery, UAV Sensors, OSINT Platforms.

 04

Physical Security

Video surveillance systems, access control hardware, perimeter intrusion detection, and security operations centers protecting commercial, government, and critical infrastructure facilities.

Includes: Video Surveillance (CCTV/IP), Access Control Systems, Perimeter Security, Alarm Monitoring.

 05

Cloud Security

Cloud-native security platforms, CASB, CSPM, workload protection, and identity management solutions securing multi-cloud and hybrid infrastructure.

Includes: CASB, CSPM, Cloud Workload Protection, Cloud IAM, Zero Trust.

 06

Autonomous & AI Defense

Autonomous weapons systems, AI-powered defense platforms, unmanned vehicles, drone swarms, and machine learning for military applications.

Includes: Autonomous Drones, AI Targeting, Unmanned Vehicles, Robotic Systems.

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on segments with compliance-driven procurement, long sales cycles, and high operational stakes. Examples: ITAR/EAR compliance management software for defense subcontractors; visitor management and access-control platforms for corporate campuses; SIEM/SOAR orchestration tools for government SOCs; guard workforce scheduling and incident reporting software for regional security firms.
