Part A — Level 1
23 Hardware

Hardware markets are segmented by end application, component type, and manufacturing model — each with distinct supply chain dependencies, design cycles, and margin structures.

We reference two types of sources: global classification standards used by trade bodies and statistical agencies, and market-intelligence research used to size segments and track technology adoption. Use these to validate market definitions before narrowing to a specific device category or geography.

How market categories are recognized How TAM is calculated
NAICS 334 — Computer & Electronic Product Manufacturing SEMI — Semiconductor Equipment & Materials International IDC — Worldwide Hardware Trackers Gartner IT Infrastructure Research IEEE — Institute of Electrical & Electronics Engineers ISIC Rev.4 Division 26 — Computer & Electronics

These categories follow NAICS 334 divisions and IDC device taxonomy — organized by form factor, deployment context, and component layer in the technology stack.

01

Semiconductors

Integrated circuits, memory chips, and power semiconductors that form the foundational components of all electronic devices.

Includes: Logic ICs, Memory (DRAM/NAND), GPUs, Power Semiconductors, Analog Chips.

 02

Consumer Devices

PCs, laptops, smartphones, tablets, wearables, and consumer electronics spanning personal productivity, entertainment, and mobile computing.

Includes: PCs & Laptops, Smartphones, Tablets, Wearables, Consumer Electronics.

 03

Servers & Data Center Infrastructure

Rack servers, blade systems, storage arrays, and power/cooling infrastructure supporting cloud, enterprise, and edge computing.

Includes: Rack & Blade Servers, Storage Systems, UPS & Cooling, Hyperscale Infrastructure.

 04

Networking Equipment

Routers, switches, access points, and telecom infrastructure hardware enabling enterprise and carrier-grade connectivity.

Includes: Enterprise Routers & Switches, Wi-Fi Access Points, 5G RAN Equipment, Firewalls.

 05

Components & Manufacturing

Electronic components, PCBs, displays, batteries, and hardware manufacturing processes spanning design, assembly, and supply chain operations.

Includes: PCBs, Displays, Batteries, Connectors, Contract Manufacturing.

 06

Industrial & IoT Hardware

Sensors, microcontrollers, gateways, and edge devices purpose-built for industrial, automotive, and smart-infrastructure applications.

Includes: Industrial IoT Sensors, Edge Gateways, Embedded Controllers, Automotive ECUs.

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on segments with recurring design cycles and supply chain complexity where software can unlock efficiency. Examples: component lifecycle management for electronics OEMs; yield optimization dashboards for semiconductor fabs; firmware update orchestration for IoT device fleets; thermal monitoring for data center operators.
