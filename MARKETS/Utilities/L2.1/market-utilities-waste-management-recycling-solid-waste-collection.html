Solid Waste Collection

Solid Waste Collection — a segment within Waste Management & Recycling (Utilities).

Market Context

Market Size$42B U.S. collection services revenue (EREF, IBISWorld)
Growth3.1% CAGR driven by population growth, contract renewals, and CPI-indexed pricing
Key PlayersWaste Management, Republic Services, GFL Environmental, Waste Connections, Casella Waste
GeographyNational; Sun Belt growth markets (TX, FL, AZ) and dense urban Northeast are highest-revenue
CDL driver shortages affect 75% of waste haulers, with turnover rates exceeding 30% annually. Automation (automated side-loaders) and route optimization technology are the primary responses, but most mid-size haulers still rely on manual dispatch and paper-based processes.

Known Pain Points

  • Driver recruitment costs $8,000-$12,000 per hire with 90-day turnover rates of 20%+ — fleet staffing is the top operational challenge
  • Route optimization for 3-stream collection (trash, recycling, organics) requires dynamic scheduling that legacy dispatch systems cannot handle
  • Municipal contract compliance reporting (diversion rates, service level metrics) is manual and error-prone across most haulers
  • Cart/container contamination monitoring lacks scalable technology — contamination charges and rejected loads cost haulers millions annually

How to Choose a Niche

Route optimization and fleet management offer the clearest ROI — fuel and labor are 65% of collection cost. Mid-size haulers (5-50 trucks) are underserved by enterprise solutions and willing to pay for SaaS tools that reduce miles driven, missed pickups, and overtime hours.
