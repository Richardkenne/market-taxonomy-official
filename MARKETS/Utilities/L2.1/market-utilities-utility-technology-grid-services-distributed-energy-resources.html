Distributed Energy Resources

DERMS platforms, virtual power plants, rooftop solar integration, EV managed charging, and behind-the-meter resource aggregation for grid services.

These sources define how this segment is classified, sized, and tracked by industry analysts and federal agencies.

Market Context

Size / Scale$7B (global)
Growth signal+15-20% YoY driven by FERC 2222, IRA incentives, and EV growth
Key playersStem, AutoGrid (Schneider), Enbala (Generac), Itron DERMS, Oracle DERMS, GE Vernova
GeographyUS (leading policy), Australia (highest rooftop solar), EU, UK, Japan
FERC Order 2222 requires all RTOs/ISOs to allow DER aggregations to participate in wholesale markets by 2026, creating a massive new market for aggregation, forecasting, and settlement platforms.

Known Pain Points

  • DERMS integration with legacy ADMS and SCADA systems from different vendors
  • Forecasting and dispatching heterogeneous DER portfolios (solar, storage, EVs, thermostats)
  • Utility-DER owner settlement and compensation across state net metering and VPP programs
  • Hosting capacity analysis for interconnecting new solar and storage without grid upgrades

How to Choose a Niche

Target utilities with 10,000+ distributed solar installations — they are struggling to manage voltage and thermal impacts and need DERMS and hosting capacity tools.
