Recycling & Materials Recovery

Recycling & Materials Recovery — a segment within Waste Management & Recycling (Utilities).

Market Context

Market Size$30B U.S. recycling and composting industry (ISRI, EPA)
Growth4.5% CAGR driven by EPR legislation, post-consumer resin demand, and organics mandates
Key PlayersWaste Management Recycle America, Republic Services, Sims Metal Management, Pratt Industries, Balcones Resources
GeographyCalifornia, Oregon, Maine, Colorado lead EPR; Northeast has highest recycling rates; Southeast lowest
EPR laws in 8+ states are shifting recycling costs from municipalities to producers, creating a new funding mechanism expected to inject $1-2B annually into recycling infrastructure. Simultaneously, AI-based robotic sorting is achieving 95%+ purity rates, transforming MRF economics.

Known Pain Points

  • Contamination rates in single-stream recycling average 25%, causing load rejections and processing cost increases of 30-50%
  • Commodity price volatility (OCC swings from $40-$200/ton) makes MRF profitability unpredictable and discourages infrastructure investment
  • EPR compliance requires producers to track packaging volumes and fund collection — most lack the data systems for accurate reporting
  • Organic waste diversion mandates (California SB 1383) require separate collection and processing infrastructure that most communities lack

How to Choose a Niche

EPR compliance and producer responsibility reporting is an emerging, well-funded need. As states adopt EPR, thousands of brand owners and packaging producers need systems to track material flows, calculate fees, and demonstrate compliance — a greenfield software opportunity.
