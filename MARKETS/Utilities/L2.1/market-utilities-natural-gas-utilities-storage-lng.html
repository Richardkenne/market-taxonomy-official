Storage & LNG

Storage & LNG — a segment within Natural Gas Utilities (Utilities).

Market Context

Market Size$22B U.S. storage and LNG terminal revenue (EIA, FERC filings)
Growth6.5% CAGR driven by LNG export capacity expansion from 14 to 25+ Bcf/d by 2028
Key PlayersCheniere Energy, Sempra LNG, Venture Global, Southern Union, Dominion Energy
GeographyGulf Coast (LNG export), Midwest salt caverns, Appalachian depleted fields, California peak-shaving
Post-Aliso Canyon, PHMSA and state regulators imposed strict well integrity and monitoring requirements on all 400+ underground storage facilities. LNG export terminals face $10B+ per facility construction costs with complex FERC/DOE permitting requirements.

Known Pain Points

  • Storage well integrity testing and monitoring under post-Aliso Canyon rules requires continuous pressure and temperature surveillance at high cost
  • LNG facility commissioning and ramp-up delays average 12-18 months, with feed gas supply coordination adding complexity
  • Seasonal storage optimization requires sophisticated modeling of injection/withdrawal cycles against volatile spot and futures pricing
  • Cryogenic equipment maintenance at LNG terminals demands specialized workforce and predictive maintenance tools to avoid unplanned outages

How to Choose a Niche

Storage well integrity monitoring is a compliance-driven, non-discretionary spend for every operator post-Aliso Canyon. Solutions for continuous wellbore monitoring, anomaly detection, and automated regulatory reporting address a universal mandate with clear penalties for non-compliance.
