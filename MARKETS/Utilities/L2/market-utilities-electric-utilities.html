Electric Utilities

Companies that generate, transmit, and distribute electrical power to residential, commercial, and industrial customers through regulated and deregulated market structures.

Segments follow EIA's value chain classification (generation, transmission, distribution) plus FERC's jurisdictional breakdown of retail operations and NERC reliability regions.

The U.S. electric utility industry generated $495 billion in revenue in 2024. The accelerating shift toward renewables, grid modernization mandates, and electrification of transport are reshaping every segment of the value chain.

How to Choose a Niche

Look for segments where aging infrastructure meets new regulatory mandates. Distribution utilities face the most urgent operational challenges as they integrate distributed energy resources, EV charging loads, and wildfire mitigation requirements simultaneously.
