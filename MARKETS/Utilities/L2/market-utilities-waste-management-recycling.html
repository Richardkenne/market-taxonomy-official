Waste Management & Recycling

Companies that collect, transport, process, recycle, and dispose of municipal solid waste, industrial waste, and hazardous materials through regulated facilities and services.

Segments follow EPA's RCRA waste hierarchy and materials management framework, aligned with EREF research classifications and ISRI commodity categories for recycled materials.

Sources

The U.S. waste management industry generates $120 billion annually. Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) laws expanding to 8+ states, China's National Sword aftermath, and landfill capacity constraints are driving consolidation and technology investment across collection, processing, and disposal.

How to Choose a Niche

Target segments where contamination rates and regulatory compliance create daily operational pain. Recycling MRFs face 25%+ contamination rates and volatile commodity prices, while collection operators struggle with route efficiency and driver shortages — both create strong demand for software and automation.
