10 Consumer Goods

Consumer goods markets are segmented by product lifecycle, purchase frequency, and distribution channel — spanning fast-moving consumables, durable goods, personal care, and specialty categories with distinct brand economics and retail dynamics.

We reference two types of sources: global classification standards used by trade bodies and statistical agencies, and market-intelligence research used to size segments and track consumer spending trends. Use these to validate market definitions before narrowing to a specific product category or geography.

01

FMCG — Food, Beverage & Household

High-frequency consumable products sold through grocery, convenience, and mass-market retail channels with rapid inventory turnover and thin margins.

Includes: Packaged Foods, Snacks, Beverages, Cleaning Products, Paper Goods.

 02

Health & Beauty (OTC & Personal Care)

Skincare, cosmetics, oral care, and over-the-counter health products driven by brand loyalty, ingredient innovation, and premiumization trends.

Includes: Skincare, Haircare, Oral Care, OTC Medicines, Cosmetics.

 03

Consumer Electronics & Durables

Long-lifecycle products including appliances, home electronics, and smart devices with higher price points and considered purchase cycles.

Includes: Home Appliances, Smart Devices, Audio/Video, Wearables, Small Electronics.

 04

Home & Furniture

Furniture, home decor, bedding, and housewares spanning flat-pack mass-market to premium design-led brands and DTC disruptors.

Includes: Furniture, Mattresses, Home Decor, Kitchenware, Storage & Organization.

 05

Toys, Games & Leisure Products

Traditional toys, board games, outdoor recreation gear, and hobby supplies shaped by seasonal demand and licensing partnerships.

Includes: Action Figures, Board Games, Outdoor Play, Hobby Kits, Licensed Merchandise.

 06

Pet Products

Pet food, treats, health supplements, and accessories driven by humanization trends and premiumization across companion animal categories.

Includes: Pet Food, Pet Health, Grooming, Toys & Accessories, Pet Tech.

How to Choose a Niche

Look for categories where purchase frequency is high but brand switching is common — that signals unmet needs. Examples: subscription replenishment platforms for household consumables; DTC ingredient-transparency tools for personal care brands; demand-forecasting SaaS for CPG companies managing SKU proliferation across retail channels.
