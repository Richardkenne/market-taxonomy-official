Part A — Level 2.1
Beverages

Non-alcoholic and alcoholic drinks — from soft drinks and bottled water to beer, wine, spirits, and the fast-growing functional beverage category.

Market Context

Market Size$2.1 trillion globally (2024, Euromonitor). Non-alcoholic ~$1.2T, alcoholic ~$900B.
Growth Rate4.1% CAGR (2024-2029). Functional drinks and RTD cocktails growing at 8-12%.
Key PlayersCoca-Cola, PepsiCo, AB InBev, Diageo, Nestle Waters, Keurig Dr Pepper, Monster Beverage, Red Bull, Danone.
GeographyNorth America and Europe dominate value; Asia-Pacific leads volume growth, especially in bottled water and tea.
Sugar taxes in 100+ countries are forcing reformulation. Simultaneously, the no/low alcohol trend is creating a $13 billion sub-category growing at 7% annually (IWSR). DTC beverage brands struggle with unit economics due to shipping weight.

Known Pain Points

  • Cold chain logistics — temperature-sensitive products require refrigerated transport and storage, adding 30-50% to distribution costs
  • Sugar and ingredient regulation — navigating reformulation to meet evolving sugar taxes while maintaining taste profiles consumers expect
  • Route-to-market complexity — beverage distribution requires managing relationships with distributors, wholesalers, and on-premise accounts simultaneously
  • Packaging sustainability pressure — single-use plastic bans and extended producer responsibility (EPR) laws requiring deposit return schemes

Sources

Euromonitor — Drinks IWSR — Low/No Alcohol

Niche Guidance

High-pain niches include beverage route-to-market optimization for independent distributors, compliance tooling for multi-market sugar tax regulations, and cold chain monitoring for DTC/subscription beverage brands.
