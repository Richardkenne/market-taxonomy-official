Part A — Level 2.1
Frozen & Refrigerated Foods

Temperature-controlled food products — frozen meals, ice cream, chilled dairy, deli, and fresh-cut produce — the fastest-growing FMCG food segment driven by convenience and improved quality.

Market Context

Market Size$410 billion globally (2024). Frozen foods ~$280B, chilled/refrigerated ~$130B.
Growth Rate5.2% CAGR (2024-2029). Frozen meals and plant-based frozen growing at 8-10%.
Key PlayersNestle, Conagra (Birds Eye, Healthy Choice), Nomad Foods (Iglo), McCain, Schwan's, Amy's Kitchen, Tattooed Chef.
GeographyNorth America leads per-capita frozen consumption. China and India are the fastest-growing cold chain markets.
The frozen food renaissance — driven by better-for-you options and improved freezing technology — reversed decades of decline. Cold chain infrastructure investment reached $340B globally in 2024 (GCCA), but last-mile frozen delivery remains the biggest pain point for grocery e-commerce.

Known Pain Points

  • Cold chain integrity — maintaining -18°C from production to consumer with temperature excursions causing $35B+ in annual frozen food waste globally
  • Energy costs — frozen storage and transport consume 3-5x more energy than ambient, directly impacting margins amid rising electricity prices
  • Last-mile delivery — frozen grocery e-commerce requires insulated packaging and time-sensitive delivery windows, increasing fulfillment costs 40-60%
  • Shelf space competition — freezer aisle real estate is fixed and expensive; new brands struggle to secure distribution against established players

Sources

Euromonitor — Frozen Food GCCA — Cold Chain Research

Niche Guidance

High-pain opportunities include cold chain temperature monitoring IoT, frozen food e-commerce fulfillment optimization, or energy management systems for cold storage facilities.
