Critical Minerals & Battery Materials

Lithium, cobalt, rare earth elements, graphite, and other critical minerals powering the energy transition — from EV battery cathodes and anodes to wind turbine magnets and defense applications.

Data from USGS Critical Minerals List, IEA Critical Minerals Report, Benchmark Mineral Intelligence, CRU Group battery materials research, and S&P Global critical minerals coverage.

Critical mineral demand is projected to grow 4-6x by 2040 under IEA net zero scenarios. Lithium demand alone will triple by 2030. Supply chain concentration (China refines 60-90% of key minerals) is driving Western onshoring initiatives worth $100B+ in committed subsidies (US IRA, EU CRM Act, Australian Critical Minerals Strategy).

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on supply chain localization — DLE lithium, rare earth separation, and graphite anode processing outside China are the highest-priority segments for Western government subsidies (IRA, EU CRM Act). Responsible cobalt sourcing serves a regulatory mandate. Silicon-graphite anodes represent the next battery material transition.
