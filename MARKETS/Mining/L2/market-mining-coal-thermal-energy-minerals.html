Coal & Thermal Energy Minerals

Thermal coal extraction, uranium mining, and energy mineral operations — supplying fuel for power generation, industrial heat, and nuclear energy systems worldwide.

Data from IEA Coal Market Report, World Nuclear Association, USGS Energy Minerals, and Wood Mackenzie energy transition research.

Sources

IEA Coal Market World Nuclear Association USGS Coal Data Wood Mackenzie

Thermal coal remains the single largest source of electricity globally (~35%), but OECD demand is declining 3-5% annually. Meanwhile, uranium prices have tripled since 2020 as governments extend nuclear plant lifetimes and plan new SMR deployments. The energy mineral landscape is bifurcating between declining fossil fuels and resurgent nuclear.

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on the nuclear renaissance — uranium ISL optimization, fuel cycle analytics, and SMR supply chain represent high-growth opportunities. Coal mine closure and rehabilitation is a $10B+ addressable market as hundreds of mines close globally. Avoid new thermal coal technology investments.
