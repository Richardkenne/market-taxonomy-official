Cobalt Mining

Primary and by-product cobalt mining from Central African copper-cobalt sedimentary deposits and nickel laterite operations — supplying lithium-ion battery cathodes, aerospace superalloys, and chemical catalysts.

Data from USGS Critical Minerals List, IEA Critical Minerals Report, Benchmark Mineral Intelligence, CRU Group battery materials research, and S&P Global critical minerals coverage.

Market Context

Size / Scale~$15B market; ~190,000 tonnes annual production
Growth signalBattery demand growing but cathode chemistry shifting toward low-cobalt (NMC811) and cobalt-free (LFP) formulations
Key playersGlencore, CMOC, ERG, Chemaf, Umicore (refining)
GeographyDRC (73%), Indonesia (5%), Russia (4%), Australia (3%), Philippines (3%)
Cobalt faces a dual challenge: extreme geographic concentration in DRC (73% of mining) creates supply risk, while cathode chemistry evolution toward LFP and high-nickel NMC reduces cobalt intensity per kWh. Artisanal mining (15-20% of DRC output) raises severe human rights concerns.

Known Pain Points

  • Artisanal and small-scale mining (ASM) child labor and safety concerns in DRC
  • Cobalt demand intensity declining as battery chemistries shift to low-cobalt and cobalt-free
  • DRC political instability and mining code changes creating investment uncertainty
  • Chinese dominance in cobalt refining (80%+) concentrating downstream value

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on supply chain localization — DLE lithium, rare earth separation, and graphite anode processing outside China are the highest-priority segments for Western government subsidies (IRA, EU CRM Act). Responsible cobalt sourcing serves a regulatory mandate. Silicon-graphite anodes represent the next battery material transition.
