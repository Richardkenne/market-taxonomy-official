Part A — Level 1
38 Mining

Mining markets are segmented by mineral type, extraction method, and value chain position — each with distinct geological risk, regulatory requirements, and commodity price exposure.

We reference two types of sources: global classification standards used by geological surveys and commodity exchanges, and market-intelligence research used to size mineral segments and track extraction economics. Use these to validate market definitions before narrowing to a specific commodity or geography.

These categories follow ISIC Section B and NAICS 21 mining divisions — organized by commodity group, from base and precious metals through energy minerals and the technology services that support extraction operations.

01

Base Metals (Copper, Zinc, Nickel)

Extraction and primary processing of industrial base metals essential for construction, electronics, and infrastructure applications worldwide.

Includes: Copper Mining, Zinc Mining, Nickel Mining, Lead Mining, Tin Mining.

 02

Precious Metals (Gold, Silver, PGMs)

Mining and refining of gold, silver, platinum group metals, and other precious minerals valued for investment, jewelry, and industrial catalysis.

Includes: Gold Mining, Silver Mining, Platinum & Palladium, Precious Metal Refining.

 03

Iron Ore & Steel Raw Materials

Iron ore extraction, coking coal production, and ferroalloy mining supplying raw inputs for the global steelmaking value chain.

Includes: Iron Ore Mining, Coking Coal, Manganese, Chromite, Ferroalloys.

 04

Coal & Thermal Energy Minerals

Thermal coal extraction, uranium mining, and other energy mineral operations supplying fuel for power generation and industrial heat.

Includes: Thermal Coal, Uranium, Peat, Energy Mineral Processing.

 05

Critical Minerals & Battery Materials

Lithium, cobalt, rare earth elements, and other critical minerals powering the energy transition, EV batteries, and advanced electronics.

Includes: Lithium, Cobalt, Rare Earth Elements, Graphite, Vanadium.

 06

Mining Technology & Services

Autonomous drilling, fleet management, mine planning software, geological modeling, and EPCM services supporting mining operations.

Includes: Autonomous Mining Equipment, Mine Planning Software, Geological Modeling, EPCM Services.

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on segments with safety-critical operations, equipment downtime costs, or ESG reporting requirements. Examples: predictive maintenance platforms for underground haul truck fleets; tailings dam monitoring and compliance software; ore-grade reconciliation tools for mid-tier gold producers; carbon accounting for Scope 1-3 emissions across mine-to-port logistics.
