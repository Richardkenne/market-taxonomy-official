Part A — Level 2.1
Raw / Undeveloped Land

Raw and undeveloped land encompasses unimproved parcels with no existing structures, utilities, or infrastructure, ranging from agricultural land and timberland to speculative investment parcels held for future development.

Market data is sourced from USDA land value surveys, the National Land Realty market reports, and AcreTrader and FarmTogether transaction data covering agricultural and undeveloped land markets.

Sources

USDA Land Values Summary National Land Realty Market Reports AcreTrader Research REALTORS Land Institute

Market Context

Size / Scale ~1.9B acres of privately owned land in the US; agricultural land alone ~$3.5T in total value; raw land transactions ~$50–80B annually across all property types
Growth signal Farmland values rising 6–12% annually in key agricultural states; recreational and hunting land appreciating; development land values driven by local entitlement and housing demand cycles
Key players John Hancock Timberland (institutional timber owner), Farmland Partners (REIT), Gladstone Land (agricultural REIT), National Land Realty (brokerage), Mossy Oak Properties (rural land brokerage)
Geography Farmland concentrated in Midwest (IA, IL, IN, KS, NE); timberland in Southeast and Pacific Northwest; speculative development land in growth corridors of Sun Belt and Mountain West
Raw land is the most information-asymmetric real estate asset class — buyers and sellers lack data on soil quality, entitlement potential, flood risk, and access, creating a multi-sided market intelligence opportunity.

Known Pain Points

  • Land valuation for raw parcels lacks comparable sales data — raw land comps are sparse, geographically dispersed, and not captured in MLS systems, making automated valuation models unreliable
  • Entitlement feasibility assessment for raw land requires simultaneous research across zoning maps, comprehensive plans, utilities availability, access roads, and environmental databases — entirely manual and multi-week
  • Title and ownership research for rural and undeveloped parcels is complex — chain of title involves multiple deeds, easements, mineral rights splits, and access issues not easily surfaced in standard title searches
  • Land ownership data is fragmented across county assessor records with inconsistent data quality, making large-scale portfolio analysis or market scanning extremely difficult for investors and brokers

How to Choose a Niche

Land data aggregation — combining parcel ownership, zoning, soil/environmental data, and transaction history into one searchable platform — is the highest-value infrastructure play. LandSearch, Landwatch, and AcreValue are early movers but leave significant gaps in data quality and analytical depth. Target land investors, developers, and agricultural lenders who are making multi-million dollar decisions with inadequate data. Alternatively, build the entitlement feasibility workflow tool for developers actively pursuing raw-to-entitled land conversion.
