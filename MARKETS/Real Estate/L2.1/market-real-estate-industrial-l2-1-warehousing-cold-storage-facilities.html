Part A — Level 2.1
Cold Storage Facilities

Cold storage facilities are temperature-controlled warehouses — including refrigerated and frozen storage — serving food and beverage, pharmaceutical, and perishable goods supply chains.

Market data is sourced from CBRE Cold Storage Market reports, GCCA (Global Cold Chain Alliance) capacity surveys, and USDA Cold Storage reports tracking commodity-level inventory.

Sources

CBRE Cold Storage Research GCCA Global Cold Chain Alliance USDA Cold Storage Reports JLL Cold Storage Outlook

Market Context

Size / Scale ~3.2B cubic feet of refrigerated warehouse capacity in the US; market value ~$40B+; investment-grade cold storage growing 20%+ annually
Growth signal Online grocery, meal kits, and pharmaceutical cold chain driving demand surge; significant supply shortage as new purpose-built facilities take 24–36 months to deliver
Key players Americold (largest publicly traded cold storage REIT), Lineage Logistics (private, largest operator globally), Preferred Freezer Services, USFC, Prologis (entering cold storage)
Geography Port-adjacent hubs (LA/Long Beach, Seattle, Newark NJ), agricultural production zones (Central Valley CA, Midwest), and major population centers for last-mile temperature-controlled delivery
Cold storage operators run facilities with extreme energy costs, FSMA food safety compliance obligations, and temperature-monitoring requirements that create a dense, high-stakes operational management problem.

Known Pain Points

  • Energy management for refrigeration systems — which account for 60–70% of facility operating costs — lacks real-time monitoring and optimization tools calibrated for cold storage thermal loads
  • FSMA (Food Safety Modernization Act) compliance requires continuous temperature logging, traceability documentation, and sanitation records — most facilities still use paper-based or basic data-logger systems
  • Inventory slotting optimization in cold storage (product placement for pick efficiency, FIFO rotation, zone temperature management) is done manually or with generic WMS tools not built for cold environments
  • Ammonia refrigeration system maintenance tracking and PSM (Process Safety Management) compliance documentation is critical and high-liability, yet managed with general-purpose maintenance software

How to Choose a Niche

Energy monitoring and optimization for refrigeration systems is the highest-ROI wedge — every 1% energy reduction on a 1M sq ft facility is $100K+ in savings. FSMA compliance documentation and temperature traceability is a regulatory mandate with no dominant software solution. Target Americold and Lineage Logistics as design partners for enterprise deals, then productize down to mid-market operators.
