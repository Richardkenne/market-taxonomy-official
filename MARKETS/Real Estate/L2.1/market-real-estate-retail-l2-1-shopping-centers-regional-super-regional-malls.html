Part A — Level 2.1
Regional & Super-Regional Malls

Regional and super-regional malls are large enclosed retail centers anchored by department stores and national retailers, typically 400,000–2M+ sq ft, serving regional trade areas of 150,000+ consumers.

Market data draws on ICSC Mall Market Reports, Green Street Advisors mall research, and Placer.ai foot traffic analytics tracking consumer visits across Class A, B, and C mall assets.

Sources

ICSC Mall Research Green Street Advisors Retail Placer.ai Foot Traffic CoStar Retail Analytics

Market Context

Size / Scale ~1,000 regional malls remain operational in the US; Class A malls (~300 properties) generating $500+ PSF sales; Class B/C malls (~700 properties) under severe distress
Growth signal Class A: strong — sales per sq ft at record highs for top malls; Class B/C: terminal decline with 200+ closures expected by 2030; redevelopment into mixed-use creating new value
Key players Simon Property Group (largest mall REIT, 200+ properties), Brookfield Asset Management (mall owner/operator), Macerich (Class A malls), Unibail-Rodamco (global), Starwood Retail (distressed buyer)
Geography Class A malls in affluent suburbs of major metros (NY, LA, SF, Chicago, Dallas); Class B/C malls across secondary and tertiary markets facing anchor department store closures
Mall operators managing the bifurcation between thriving Class A assets and distressed Class B/C properties need data analytics, redevelopment planning tools, and tenant-mix optimization capabilities that no current platform provides.

Known Pain Points

  • Tenant sales reporting — the foundation of percentage rent collection and lease compliance — is submitted manually by retailers on paper forms or Excel, with no automated integration into POS systems
  • Foot traffic and consumer behavior analytics are purchased from third parties (Placer.ai, Buxton) with no integration into leasing or tenant management workflows, making data-driven leasing decisions slow
  • Redevelopment planning for distressed mall properties (converting anchor boxes to residential, medical, or entertainment uses) requires simultaneous legal, financial, and design analysis across dozens of stakeholders
  • Specialty leasing and temporary tenant management (pop-ups, kiosks, seasonal retail) for the non-anchor GLA is tracked in spreadsheets with no pipeline or revenue management system

How to Choose a Niche

Avoid building for distressed Class B/C malls — operators lack budget and assets are in workout. Focus on Class A mall operators (Simon, Macerich, Brookfield) and target the tenant sales reporting and analytics layer — it is universally manual and has direct NOI impact. Alternatively, build the redevelopment feasibility tool for mall-to-mixed-use conversion, which is an active $50B+ capital allocation decision being made across hundreds of assets.
