Part A — Level 2.1
Flex Industrial Properties

Flex industrial properties are versatile single-story buildings combining warehouse or light industrial space with office, showroom, or R&D space, typically leased to small and mid-size businesses in multi-tenant parks.

Market data draws on NAIOP flex industrial surveys, CoStar flex space analytics, and Marcus & Millichap small-bay industrial research tracking vacancy, rents, and tenant profiles.

Sources

NAIOP Industrial Research Marcus & Millichap Flex Industrial CoStar Flex Space Analytics CBRE Small-Bay Industrial

Market Context

Size / Scale ~4–5B sq ft of flex and small-bay industrial in the US; represents ~25–30% of total industrial inventory; dominated by buildings under 50,000 sq ft
Growth signal Vacancy at record lows (3–5%) in most markets; rent growth of 8–15% over past 3 years; e-commerce contractors, tradespeople, and small manufacturers driving demand
Key players EastGroup Properties (REIT, Sun Belt focus), Highwoods Properties (mixed flex/office), Link Logistics (Blackstone, multi-tenant focus), Plymouth Industrial REIT, and numerous regional private owners
Geography Ubiquitous across all US metros in suburban industrial parks; strongest demand in Sun Belt (Phoenix, Dallas, Atlanta, Tampa) and infill urban markets; supply constrained in most major metros
Flex industrial parks with 20–100 small-bay tenants have property management complexity similar to retail strip centers but with industrial operational needs — and almost no purpose-built software.

Known Pain Points

  • Tenant mix management for flex parks (tracking contractor licenses, business type, zoning compliance, and neighbor use compatibility) is done manually and creates liability when incompatible uses are co-located
  • Work order and maintenance management for multi-tenant flex parks covering roof, dock seals, HVAC, and exterior maintenance across dozens of units has no purpose-built platform — operators use generic tools poorly
  • Lease renewal tracking and proactive outreach for small-business tenants (who often operate informally) is passive — operators miss renewal windows and lose tenants to competing parks
  • Utility submetering and billing for multi-tenant flex buildings with variable industrial power consumption is inaccurate and creates tenant disputes that standard utility billing software cannot resolve

How to Choose a Niche

Flex industrial park management software is a clear whitespace — no dominant player exists for the 20–200 unit multi-tenant park operator. Start with the lease management and tenant communication layer (easiest to sell), then expand into maintenance and utility billing. Target regional owners of 5–20 flex parks — they have enough scale to feel the pain and enough budget to pay for a solution.
