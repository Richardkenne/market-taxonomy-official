Medical Office Buildings

Medical office buildings (MOBs) are purpose-built or healthcare-adapted commercial properties housing physician practices, outpatient clinics, imaging centers, and ancillary health services.

Market data draws on CBRE Healthcare Real Estate reports, Marcus & Millichap MOB research, and HREI (Healthcare Real Estate Insights) investment and leasing surveys.

Sources

CBRE Healthcare Real Estate Marcus & Millichap MOB Research HREI Healthcare Real Estate Insights Cushman & Wakefield Healthcare

Market Context

Size / Scale ~1.4B sq ft of MOB inventory in the US; ~$180B asset value; investment sales volume ~$10–15B annually
Growth signal Outperforming traditional office; 4–5% cap rate compression over past decade; aging population driving outpatient visit volume growth of 3–5% annually
Key players Healthcare Trust (REIT), Physicians Realty Trust / Healthpeak (REIT), Duke Realty (now Prologis), CBRE Healthcare (brokerage/management), Hammes (developer)
Geography Concentrated in suburban medical corridors near hospitals and health systems; strongest markets: Sun Belt, Midwest (Chicago, Minneapolis), and Southeast (Atlanta, Charlotte, Nashville)
MOB operators manage tenants who are medical practices with unique buildout, HIPAA, ADA, and operational requirements — creating a compliance and operations management surface that generic commercial tools cannot address.

Known Pain Points

  • Tenant buildout and tenant improvement (TI) management for medical suites is complex — MEP requirements, plumbing, medical gas, and ADA compliance make standard TI tracking tools inadequate
  • Lease management for healthcare tenants involves percentage rent based on practice revenue, co-tenancy clauses with anchor health systems, and regulatory use restrictions that standard lease admin software mishandles
  • Infection control and clinical-grade cleaning protocols for MOBs require documentation and scheduling systems distinct from standard janitorial management
  • HVAC and air quality monitoring for exam rooms, procedure suites, and labs operates under different standards than commercial office, with no specialized FM software category to serve it

How to Choose a Niche

Start with property managers who specialize in MOBs (CBRE Healthcare, JLL Healthcare divisions, regional specialists) — they manage dozens of properties with standardized needs. Tenant improvement project management for medical buildouts is the clearest whitespace. Lease abstraction and administration for healthcare-specific clauses is a durable, recurring revenue wedge.
