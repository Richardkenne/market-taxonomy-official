Senior & Assisted Living

Senior and assisted living encompasses age-restricted communities, independent living, assisted living facilities (ALFs), memory care, and continuing care retirement communities (CCRCs) serving adults 55+.

Market sizing uses NIC MAP data on senior housing inventory and occupancy, combined with ALFA/Argentum operator surveys and CMS care quality databases.

Sources

NIC MAP Senior Housing Data Argentum Industry Data CMS Care Compare JLL Senior Housing Research

Market Context

Size / Scale ~$475B senior housing and care market; ~30,000 ALF communities; 1M+ assisted living beds nationally; NIC tracks ~15,000 investment-grade properties
Growth signal Occupancy recovering past 87% post-COVID; 73M Baby Boomers entering prime senior housing age 2025–2035; supply under-building vs. demographic demand curve
Key players Brookdale Senior Living (largest operator, 650+ communities), Sunrise Senior Living (premium operator), Welltower (REIT, largest owner), Ventas (REIT), Life Care Services (CCRC manager)
Geography Sun Belt and Southeast dominant (FL, AZ, TX); suburban markets outperforming urban; secondary markets seeing development as primary markets saturate
Senior living operators sit at the intersection of real estate and healthcare, creating a uniquely complex operating environment where staffing, care coordination, and occupancy management all fail simultaneously — massive tooling opportunity.

Known Pain Points

  • Staffing and scheduling for 24/7 care teams is the top operational burden — most facilities use manual shift-swapping and paper schedules, driving overtime costs and turnover above 60% annually
  • Move-in and move-out coordination requires simultaneous management of healthcare assessments, family communication, unit preparation, and financial onboarding — entirely manual at most operators
  • Family communication and care transparency tools are primitive — most families receive phone calls or paper reports, with no digital portal for care updates, billing, or activity participation
  • Census and occupancy management lacks predictive tooling — operators cannot model lead-to-move-in conversion rates or forecast occupancy 60–90 days out with any precision

How to Choose a Niche

Focus on the operator segment (25–200 unit independent or small-chain operators) who are priced out of enterprise platforms like PointClickCare and MatrixCare. Family communication and staffing scheduling are the two entry wedges with fastest sales cycles. Regulatory documentation (state survey prep, incident reporting) is a high-value, defensible niche with direct liability implications for operators.
