Part A — Level 2.1
Short-Term / Vacation Rentals

Short-term and vacation rentals are privately owned residential properties rented for periods under 30 days, distributed through platforms like Airbnb, Vrbo, and Booking.com, and managed by individual hosts or professional property managers.

Market data is sourced from AirDNA's global STR supply and revenue tracking, VRMA industry surveys, and Phocuswright travel accommodation research.

Market Context

Size / Scale ~$100B+ global STR market; 1M+ active listings in the US alone; ~$50B US market revenue; average host earns ~$14K/yr
Growth signal Post-COVID normalization; professional management growing as individual hosts exit; regulatory headwinds in major cities creating supply contraction and yield improvement
Key players Airbnb (distribution platform), Vrbo/Expedia (OTA), Vacasa (professional manager, 35K+ homes), Evolve (hybrid management), Guesty & Hostaway (PMS platforms)
Geography Beach and mountain leisure destinations dominant (FL, CO, NC Outer Banks, Hawaii); urban markets facing increasing regulation; rural/drive-to markets expanding post-COVID
Professional STR managers operating 50–500 units across multiple OTAs face revenue optimization, dynamic pricing, and maintenance coordination problems that no single platform solves end-to-end.

Known Pain Points

  • Dynamic pricing across Airbnb, Vrbo, and Booking.com simultaneously requires either expensive tools (Beyond, Wheelhouse) or manual rate management — small managers cannot afford or navigate these
  • Turnover cleaning and inspection coordination between guests — sometimes with 4-hour windows — is the single biggest operational failure point, with no real-time status visibility for managers
  • Regulatory compliance (local STR permits, occupancy tax remittance, HOA restrictions) varies by municipality and is tracked manually, creating compliance risk and fines for portfolio operators
  • Guest communication across multiple channels (Airbnb messaging, SMS, email) is fragmented, making response-time SLA management and review generation inconsistent

How to Choose a Niche

Target professional managers operating 20–300 units — they have budget, feel real operational pain, and are underserved by both enterprise and consumer tools. Operations automation (cleaning coordination, maintenance dispatch, guest messaging) has more durable value than pricing, which is commoditizing. Alternatively, build for a specific geography or property type (beach condos, mountain cabins) and own that vertical deeply.
