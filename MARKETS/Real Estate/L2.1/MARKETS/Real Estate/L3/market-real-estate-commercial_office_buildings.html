Part A — Level 2.1
Office Buildings

Office buildings are purpose-built or converted commercial properties providing workspace for businesses, ranging from suburban Class B buildings to downtown Class A trophy towers.

Market data draws on CBRE, JLL, and Cushman & Wakefield office market reports, supplemented by CoStar availability rate tracking and BOMA building operations surveys.

Sources

CBRE Office Market Reports JLL Office Outlook Cushman & Wakefield Office Research BOMA Experience Exchange Report

Market Context

Size / Scale ~11B sq ft of office space in the US; ~$2.5T total asset value; national availability rate at record highs of 20–22% post-2020
Growth signal Structural decline in demand; flight-to-quality with Class A seeing positive net absorption while Class B/C struggles; office-to-residential conversion creating new activity
Key players Brookfield Asset Management (owner/developer), SL Green (NYC REIT), Boston Properties (Class A REIT), CBRE (brokerage/management), JLL (brokerage/management)
Geography Gateway cities (NYC, SF, Chicago, Boston, DC) hardest hit by remote work; Sun Belt metros (Dallas, Austin, Miami, Atlanta) showing stronger demand; suburban markets mixed
With record vacancy and pressure to attract tenants back, office landlords and operators are investing heavily in tenant experience, space utilization data, and building technology — creating durable demand for proptech.

Known Pain Points

  • Space utilization and occupancy data is unavailable or unreliable — landlords cannot demonstrate to tenants how space is used or make data-driven decisions about reconfiguration
  • Tenant experience apps are siloed per building and poorly adopted — most achieve under 20% active user rates among building occupants
  • Lease abstracting and CAM reconciliation for multi-tenant office buildings remains largely manual, with significant liability for billing errors on complex NNN leases
  • Building access control, HVAC scheduling, and amenity booking systems are from different vendors with no integration, creating operational inefficiency and poor tenant UX

How to Choose a Niche

Avoid broad office software given structural demand headwinds. Instead, target the office-to-residential conversion workflow (due diligence, permitting, pro forma analysis) which is an active market. Alternatively, focus on tenant experience for Class A buildings — landlords are spending on amenities and apps to retain tenants in a competitive leasing environment.
