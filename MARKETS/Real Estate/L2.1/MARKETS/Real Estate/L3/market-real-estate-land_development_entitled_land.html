Part A — Level 2.1
Entitled Land

Entitled land refers to parcels that have received government approvals (zoning, subdivision, environmental clearances, and development permits) allowing construction to begin, representing the highest-value segment of the development land market.

Market data is sourced from NAHB entitled lot surveys, John Burns Real Estate Consulting lot inventory tracking, and homebuilder and commercial developer public filings on entitled land pipeline values.

Sources

NAHB Lot Supply Quarterly John Burns Lot Pipeline Research Zonda Entitled Land Analytics CoStar Entitled Land Transactions

Market Context

Size / Scale Entitled residential lots represent ~$150B+ in aggregate value nationally; entitled commercial land premium is 40–200% over raw land value in most markets; entitled industrial parcels command $30–100 PSF in top markets
Growth signal Entitled land supply critically short in most growth markets; 3–7 year entitlement timelines creating significant land banking opportunities; entitled lot prices rising 10–25% annually in constrained markets
Key players Forestar Group (D.R. Horton subsidiary, entitled residential lots), Richfield Real Estate (land banking), Taylor Morrison (vertical entitlement integration), LandBank Capital (private), Schuler Homes (finished lot supply)
Geography Highest-value entitled lots in coastal California, NYC metro, Seattle, and DC; highest-volume entitled lot markets in Dallas-Fort Worth, Phoenix, Atlanta, Charlotte, and Houston
Entitled land is the rarest and most valuable form of development land, yet the entitlement process that creates it is managed with the least sophisticated tooling — a workflow ripe for disruption.

Known Pain Points

  • Entitlement status tracking across a portfolio of parcels in various stages (pre-application, planning commission, appeals, permit issuance) has no purpose-built database — developers use Excel and shared drives
  • Municipal approval process management involves coordinating engineers, attorneys, planners, and public agency staff across dozens of submission deadlines with no shared project management platform
  • Condition of approval (COA) tracking — monitoring and clearing the specific conditions attached to entitlement approvals before building permits can be issued — is entirely manual and creates closing delays
  • Entitled land valuation is highly localized and time-sensitive — comparable entitled lot sales data is sparse, not captured in MLS systems, and requires broker network intelligence to price accurately

How to Choose a Niche

Entitlement project management software is a clear, underserved opportunity — every residential and commercial developer manages entitlements manually with spreadsheets and shared drives. Build a workflow tool covering permit tracking, condition of approval management, and agency correspondence logging. Target regional homebuilders and land developers with 10–100 active entitlement projects — they have the most acute pain and clearest ROI from improved timeline management.
