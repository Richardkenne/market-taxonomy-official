Part A — Level 2.1
Student Housing

Student housing encompasses purpose-built and off-campus rental housing serving college and university students, characterized by by-the-bed leasing, high turnover, and proximity to campuses.

Market data is drawn from JLL Student Housing reports, Axiometrics/RealPage campus analytics, and National Multifamily Housing Council enrollment-housing correlation studies.

Sources

JLL Student Housing Research RealPage Campus Analytics NMHC Student Housing NCHEMS Enrollment Data

Market Context

Size / Scale ~$10B annual investment market; ~8M purpose-built beds nationally; 19M+ enrolled students in the US
Growth signal ~3–4% annual rent growth for purpose-built; international student enrollment driving demand recovery post-COVID; supply constrained near top-50 universities
Key players American Campus Communities (largest REIT), Greystar (operator & developer), Landmark Properties (developer), Asset Living (third-party manager), Corvias (P3 specialist)
Geography Clustered within 1 mile of major university campuses; strongest markets: Austin TX, Gainesville FL, College Station TX, Athens GA, Ann Arbor MI, Champaign IL
Student housing operates on a compressed annual leasing cycle with high turnover, by-the-bed complexity, and parental co-signer dynamics that standard multifamily software handles poorly.

Known Pain Points

  • By-the-bed leasing with individual leases per room — not per unit — creates billing, maintenance, and roommate-matching complexity that generic property management software cannot handle
  • Annual turnover of near 100% compresses leasing, move-out inspection, and unit-turn workflows into a 6–8 week summer window with severe staffing and coordination pressure
  • Parental guarantor management and co-signer workflows are entirely manual, with DocuSign cobbled onto legacy systems and no automated reminder or tracking infrastructure
  • Amenity and community programming (study rooms, fitness, dining) bookings are managed through disconnected tools with no integration into the resident experience platform

How to Choose a Niche

Target third-party student housing managers (Asset Living, Greystar student division) who manage dozens of properties across multiple universities — they feel the tooling pain acutely and have budget. The by-the-bed leasing engine and annual-cycle turn management are the two highest-leverage wedges. Avoid building a full PMS; integrate into Entrata or Yardi and own a specific workflow.
