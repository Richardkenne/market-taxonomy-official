Part A — Level 2.1
Industrial Development Land

Industrial development land encompasses parcels zoned for warehouse, distribution, manufacturing, or flex industrial use, acquired by industrial developers and REITs for speculative and build-to-suit development.

Market data draws on CBRE Industrial Land reports, Prologis land acquisition disclosures, and JLL Industrial development pipeline analyses tracking shovel-ready industrial land across major logistics markets.

Sources

CBRE Industrial Land Research Prologis Development Pipeline JLL Industrial Development Outlook NAIOP Development Survey

Market Context

Size / Scale Industrial land sales ~$15–25B annually; land prices for industrial parcels ranging from $3–$5 PSF (Midwest) to $50–$150 PSF (Inland Empire, NJ) in constrained infill markets
Growth signal Unprecedented demand — shovel-ready industrial land in primary markets essentially sold out; developers pursuing brownfield redevelopment and secondary market expansion; reshoring adding new demand nodes
Key players Prologis (largest acquirer of industrial land globally), Panattoni (largest private industrial developer), IDI Logistics, Hillwood (Perot Enterprises), Duke Realty (now Prologis), and Becknell Industrial
Geography Primary markets land-constrained (Inland Empire CA, NJ, Chicago); secondary growth markets: Phoenix, Dallas, Savannah GA, Kansas City, Indianapolis, Columbus OH; port-adjacent and interstate locations command premiums
Industrial land scarcity has made land acquisition and site selection the critical competitive differentiator for industrial developers — creating demand for market intelligence, entitlement tracking, and pipeline management tools.

Known Pain Points

  • Shovel-ready industrial land identification in target markets requires simultaneous analysis of zoning, utility capacity (power, water, sewer), truck access, rail availability, and flood plain status — no tool integrates all these data layers
  • Brownfield redevelopment feasibility for industrial reuse requires Phase 1 and Phase 2 environmental assessment management, remediation cost estimation, and regulatory approval tracking across EPA and state agencies
  • Industrial land entitlement — rezoning, site plan approval, and infrastructure agreements — in supply-constrained markets requires managing multi-year approval timelines with multiple municipal agencies
  • Speculative industrial development land pipeline management across multiple simultaneous projects (site selection, due diligence, entitlement, construction) has no integrated project tracking platform for mid-size developers

How to Choose a Niche

Industrial site selection and land intelligence platform is the highest-value opportunity — Prologis has built internal tools, but mid-size industrial developers (20–50 active projects) have nothing comparable. Data on available industrial-zoned parcels, power/utility capacity, and entitlement status in key markets is extremely fragmented and valuable. Target industrial developers and their site acquisition teams directly, then expand to economic development agencies as a second channel.
