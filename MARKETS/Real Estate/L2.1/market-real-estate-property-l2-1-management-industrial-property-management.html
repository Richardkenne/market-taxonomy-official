Industrial Property Management

Industrial property management covers the operational oversight of warehouses, distribution centers, flex industrial parks, and manufacturing facilities on behalf of owners, encompassing maintenance, tenant services, lease administration, and capital planning.

Data is sourced from BOMA Industrial Experience Exchange Reports, NAIOP industrial management surveys, and Prologis and EastGroup Properties operating expense disclosures.

Sources

BOMA Industrial Research NAIOP Industrial Management Prologis Investor Relations EastGroup Properties Research

Market Context

Size / Scale Industrial PM market ~$8–10B annual revenue; growing with industrial REIT portfolios; third-party industrial PM less common than in commercial — most large owners self-manage
Growth signal Growing as industrial portfolios expand; outsourcing to third-party managers increasing among private equity owners; ESG and sustainability requirements adding new management complexity
Key players CBRE Industrial Management, JLL Industrial, Cushman & Wakefield Industrial, Colliers International, regional industrial specialists in major distribution markets
Geography Aligned with industrial market hubs: Inland Empire CA, Dallas-Fort Worth, Chicago, Atlanta, NJ/PA corridor, Memphis, Louisville, Phoenix
Industrial property management is a relatively lean operation — fewer tenants, simpler buildouts — but portfolio scale and specialized infrastructure (docks, cranes, HVAC for large clear heights) create maintenance tracking and CapEx planning challenges that generic tools fail to address.

Known Pain Points

  • Preventive maintenance scheduling for dock levelers, dock seals, overhead doors, and large-format HVAC systems across a portfolio of dozens of industrial buildings lacks purpose-built CMMS features for industrial-specific equipment
  • Tenant-responsible maintenance tracking under NNN leases (where tenants self-maintain HVAC, roof, etc.) requires landlord oversight documentation that standard property management software does not support
  • CapEx planning and reserve modeling for industrial roof systems, concrete parking and dock aprons, and exterior lighting across large portfolios involves complex per-square-foot cost models not built into PM software
  • Sustainability and energy reporting for industrial portfolios — LED retrofits, EV charging, solar installations, and ENERGY STAR certifications — is aggregated manually from utility data and contractor invoices

How to Choose a Niche

Industrial property management software is the least-served property type — Yardi and MRI are built for multifamily and office, with industrial bolted on. Build an industrial-specific operations platform (maintenance scheduling, NNN compliance tracking, CapEx planning) and target private equity-owned industrial portfolios of 20–100 buildings where the institutional owner demands reporting quality that self-managed Excel-based systems cannot deliver.
