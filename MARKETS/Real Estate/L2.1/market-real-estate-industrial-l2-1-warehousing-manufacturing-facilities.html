Manufacturing Facilities

Manufacturing facilities are heavy industrial properties purpose-built or adapted for production, assembly, and fabrication operations, featuring heavy power, crane access, high clear heights, and specialized infrastructure.

Data sources include NAIOP heavy industrial surveys, CBRE Manufacturing & Industrial reports, and Reshoring Initiative data tracking domestic manufacturing facility investment and job announcements.

Sources

Reshoring Initiative Data CBRE Manufacturing Real Estate NAIOP Industrial Research Site Selection Magazine

Market Context

Size / Scale ~4B sq ft of manufacturing space in the US; $200B+ in announced manufacturing investments 2022–2025 driven by CHIPS Act, IRA, and reshoring trends
Growth signal Strongest growth in a generation; semiconductor fabs, EV battery plants, and defense manufacturing driving mega-project activity; site selection consulting booming
Key players Duke Realty (now Prologis), Panattoni (build-to-suit developer), Seefried Industrial (developer), CBRE Site Selection (advisory), Cushman & Wakefield Industrial (advisory)
Geography Auto corridor (MI, OH, IN, KY, TN); semiconductor clusters (AZ Phoenix, NY Albany, TX); EV battery belt (GA, NC, TN, KY); defense manufacturing (TX, VA, AL, CT)
Manufacturing facility development and operation involves complex build-to-suit procurement, specialized infrastructure management, and long-term lease structures that create real estate management problems distinct from standard commercial.

Known Pain Points

  • Build-to-suit manufacturing facility project management spans 3–7 years from site selection to occupancy — no integrated platform manages the full lifecycle from site acquisition through construction completion and lease-up
  • Heavy infrastructure management (cranes, high-voltage electrical, compressed air, water treatment, rail access) within manufacturing facilities requires specialized CMMS features that generic tools lack
  • Environmental compliance tracking (stormwater, air permits, hazardous materials storage) for manufacturing tenants creates landlord liability documentation requirements handled entirely outside property management software
  • Sale-leaseback structures for manufacturing facilities (common when manufacturers seek capital) require specialized underwriting, lease structuring, and ongoing compliance monitoring tools

How to Choose a Niche

Site selection analytics for manufacturing is a $2B+ consulting industry ripe for software disruption — data on labor markets, utility costs, tax incentives, and infrastructure is fragmented across dozens of public sources. Build a site selection intelligence platform and sell to economic development agencies, corporate real estate teams, and industrial brokers. Alternatively, focus on the build-to-suit project management workflow, which has no dominant software player.
