Part A — Level 2.1
Corporate Campuses

Corporate campuses are large, owner-occupied or leased multi-building office and R&D complexes operated by major corporations, typically featuring on-site amenities, transportation, and facilities management at scale.

Data draws on CBRE Corporate Real Estate surveys, CoreNet Global occupier research, and Gartner workplace technology adoption studies covering Fortune 1000 real estate portfolios.

Sources

CoreNet Global Research CBRE Occupier Research JLL Future of Work IFMA Research

Market Context

Size / Scale Fortune 1000 companies collectively occupy ~3B+ sq ft; corporate real estate spend ~$800B+ globally including leasing, FM, and construction
Growth signal Hybrid work driving campus redesign and space consolidation; sustainability mandates and energy retrofits generating new capital spend; emerging market campus development accelerating
Key players JLL (integrated FM & CRE services), CBRE (FM & advisory), ISS (FM outsourcing), Cushman & Wakefield (corporate services), Brookfield (campus owner/developer for tech/biotech)
Geography Silicon Valley (Apple, Google, Meta, Nvidia), Seattle/Bellevue (Amazon, Microsoft), Austin, Boston Route 128 corridor, Research Triangle NC; pharmaceutical campuses in NJ and CT
Corporate real estate teams managing millions of square feet across hybrid work environments need portfolio analytics, space planning, and facilities management tools that can handle enterprise complexity at campus scale.

Known Pain Points

  • Space utilization measurement across hybrid work schedules is unreliable — badge data, sensor data, and desk booking data live in separate systems with no unified dashboard for portfolio decisions
  • Facilities maintenance work order management across thousands of assets (HVAC units, elevators, restrooms, cafeteria equipment) on a single campus lacks predictive maintenance capabilities
  • Sustainability reporting (energy, water, waste, Scope 1 and 2 emissions) for campus portfolios requires manual aggregation from utility bills and building management systems with no automated pipeline
  • Move management and space reconfiguration coordination for large campus reconfigurations involves 20+ vendors and stakeholders with no shared project management platform

How to Choose a Niche

Enterprise corporate campus is dominated by IBM TRIRIGA, Archibus, and FM:Systems. The opportunity is vertical-specific analytics on top of these platforms: sustainability reporting, space utilization dashboards, or predictive maintenance models. Target the corporate real estate director at companies with 500K–5M sq ft of owned or leased space — they have discretionary budget and are actively replacing legacy tools.
