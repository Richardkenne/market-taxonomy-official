Part A — Level 2.1
Multi-Family Residential

Multi-family residential encompasses apartment buildings, condominiums, and complexes with two or more dwelling units, serving renters and owner-occupants across urban and suburban markets.

Market sizing draws on NMHC, CoStar, and Census Bureau data tracking rental unit supply, absorption rates, and development pipelines across major metro areas.

Sources

NMHC Research CoStar Multifamily Census American Community Survey RealPage Analytics

Market Context

Size / Scale ~44 million renter-occupied units in the US; multifamily construction starts ~400K–500K units/yr
Growth signal Rent growth moderating to 2–3% nationally after 2021–2022 surge; Sun Belt markets absorbing new supply
Key players AvalonBay (REIT, large-scale development & ownership), Greystar (management & development), Equity Residential (REIT, urban focus), MAA (Sun Belt REIT)
Geography Concentrated in major metros: NYC, LA, Dallas, Houston, Atlanta, Denver, Seattle, and Miami; Sun Belt driving new supply
Multi-family operators manage hundreds to thousands of units with fragmented tooling for leasing, maintenance, resident experience, and revenue management — creating recurring software and services opportunities.

Known Pain Points

  • Revenue management is manual or relies on expensive legacy platforms (Yardi, RealPage) with poor UX and high switching costs
  • Maintenance coordination across large portfolios lacks real-time visibility — work orders fall through cracks between residents, staff, and vendors
  • Lease renewal and resident retention workflows are underdeveloped, leading to avoidable vacancy and turnover costs of $1,000–$3,000 per unit
  • Utility submetering, RUBS billing, and utility expense management remain highly manual and error-prone at scale

How to Choose a Niche

Target by unit count tier: boutique operators (10–100 units) are underserved by enterprise platforms; mid-market (100–1,000 units) are price-sensitive and switching-ready. Alternatively, focus on a workflow vertical — revenue management, maintenance, or resident communications — rather than an all-in-one platform play.
