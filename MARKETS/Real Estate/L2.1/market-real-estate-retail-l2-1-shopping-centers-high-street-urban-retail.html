Part A — Level 2.1
High-Street / Urban Retail

High-street and urban retail encompasses ground-floor commercial space on major urban shopping corridors — including flagship stores, luxury boutiques, and experiential retailers — in dense city neighborhoods.

Market data is sourced from CBRE Urban Retail reports, JLL High Street Index, and Cushman & Wakefield Main Street reports tracking ground-floor vacancy and rent trends on major urban corridors.

Sources

CBRE Urban Retail Report Cushman & Wakefield Main Streets Report JLL Retail Outlook CoStar Urban Retail Analytics

Market Context

Size / Scale Urban ground-floor retail in top 10 US cities represents ~200M+ sq ft; rents on top corridors range from $300–$3,000 PSF (NYC Fifth Avenue, Rodeo Drive); total market value $100B+
Growth signal Recovery uneven — luxury flagships and experiential retail strong; mid-tier fashion soft; remote work reducing weekday foot traffic in office-dependent corridors like NYC Midtown
Key players L Brands, LVMH, Uniqlo (flagship tenants); SL Green, Vornado Realty Trust (NYC landlords); Brookfield Properties (urban retail owner); Thor Equities (urban retail specialist buyer)
Geography NYC (Fifth Ave, SoHo, Madison Ave), Chicago (Magnificent Mile), LA (Rodeo Drive, Abbott Kinney), SF (Union Square), Miami (Lincoln Road, Wynwood), Boston (Newbury St)
Urban retail landlords managing high-profile ground-floor space on major corridors need tenant pipeline analytics, market intelligence, and lease management tools calibrated for the deal complexity and brand value of flagship retail.

Known Pain Points

  • Tenant prospecting and brand outreach for flagship urban retail spaces requires understanding brand expansion strategies and store rollout plans — no tool aggregates this intelligence for landlord leasing teams
  • Pop-up and short-term retail lease management for urban corridors (flexible 3–12 month terms as brands test markets) is managed with bespoke legal agreements and no standardized platform
  • Foot traffic and consumer demographic analysis for specific urban blocks varies dramatically and is expensive to obtain — landlords cannot easily benchmark their corridor against competitive streets
  • Retail real estate deals on major urban corridors are highly relationship-driven with long negotiating timelines — deal pipeline and landlord-broker relationship management lacks any CRM purpose-built for retail leasing

How to Choose a Niche

Target urban retail-focused landlords (Thor Equities, Vornado, SL Green retail divisions) with a leasing intelligence and tenant pipeline product. Brand expansion tracking — aggregating retailer store opening announcements, lease expirations, and expansion signals — is the unique data wedge that no current tool provides. Short-term and pop-up retail management (standardized lease templates, payment automation, foot traffic reporting) is a growing sub-market with no adequate software.
