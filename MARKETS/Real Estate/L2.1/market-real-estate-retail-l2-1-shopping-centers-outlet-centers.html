Part A — Level 2.1
Outlet Centers

Outlet centers are open-air or enclosed retail destinations where brand manufacturers sell merchandise directly to consumers at discounted prices, typically located in drive-to tourist or highway-adjacent markets.

Market data draws on ICSC outlet center reports, Premium Outlets and Tanger Factory Outlet investor disclosures, and Placer.ai consumer visit data tracking outlet center performance.

Sources

ICSC Outlet Center Research Tanger Outlets Investor Relations Simon Premium Outlets Placer.ai Retail Foot Traffic

Market Context

Size / Scale ~200 outlet centers in the US; ~75M sq ft of outlet GLA; ~$30B in annual consumer sales; concentrated among a few major operators
Growth signal Value-seeking consumer behavior driving strong sales growth; international tourism to outlet destinations rebounding; limited new supply due to high development cost and site requirements
Key players Simon Property Group / Premium Outlets (largest, 80+ centers), Tanger Factory Outlet (REIT, 35+ centers), Brookfield (joint ventures), Nuveen Real Estate (investor)
Geography Highway-adjacent drive-to markets within 30–60 miles of major metros; iconic destinations: Woodbury Common NY, Wrentham MA, Las Vegas NV, Orlando FL, Cabazon CA
Outlet center operators manage brand-manufacturer tenants with unique direct-to-consumer operational requirements, tourist-driven foot traffic patterns, and multi-channel brand relationships that differ fundamentally from standard retail.

Known Pain Points

  • Brand sales reporting for outlet tenants — required for percentage rent and trade area analysis — is submitted manually with no POS integration, creating data lags of weeks or months
  • Visitor origin and tourism demand analytics (critical for marketing spend allocation and new center site selection) are purchased from multiple third-party sources with no unified dashboard
  • Tourism and group sales coordination (bus tours, international group bookings, VIP shopping experiences) is managed through ad hoc email and phone with no CRM or booking platform
  • Multi-brand tenant coordination for center-wide promotional events (Friends and Family weekends, holiday campaigns) requires synchronizing dozens of brand marketing teams with no shared planning tool

How to Choose a Niche

The outlet center market is highly concentrated (Simon and Tanger dominate) — selling to them requires enterprise sales motions. Target the tenant-side problem instead: brand outlet managers (Nike, Gap, Coach) who operate 50–200 outlet locations across multiple landlord portfolios need performance analytics and lease management tools that landlord platforms do not provide. This is an underserved, well-funded buyer segment.
