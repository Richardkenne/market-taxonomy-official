Part C — Delivery Models

12 Delivery / Operating Models

The finite set of structural patterns through which value reaches the user — across digital, physical, and service-based businesses, independent of market or era.

Foundation Why Delivery Models Are Universal & Orthogonal to Business Systems The theory, the sources, and the distinction that matters.

Digital delivery

01 — SaaS / Cloud

SaaS / Cloud Software

Multi-tenant software accessed via browser. No installation, continuous updates. Examples: Salesforce, Notion, Slack.

02 — API-First

API-First / Embedded Digital

Value consumed programmatically — UI optional. Built to integrate into other products. Examples: Stripe, Twilio, OpenAI API.

03 — On-Premise

On-Premise / Installed Software

Deployed in the customer's own environment. Maximum control, full IT responsibility. Examples: SAP on-prem, Oracle DB.

04 — Open-Core

Open-Core + Hosted

Open-source core with commercial hosting or enterprise layers. Community drives adoption; hosted tier captures revenue. Examples: GitLab, Elastic, HashiCorp.

Physical delivery

05 — Direct Physical (D2C)

Direct Physical / D2C

Goods manufactured or sourced and shipped directly to the end customer, bypassing intermediaries. Examples: hair brands, ornament manufacturers, DTC food brands.

06 — Retail / In-store

Retail / In-store

Products sold through physical retail locations — owned stores or third-party retailers. Examples: consumer goods, fashion, construction materials.

07 — On-demand / Last-mile

On-demand / Last-mile

Rapid physical delivery triggered by real-time order. Speed is the core proposition. Examples: food delivery, q-commerce, courier networks.

08 — Wholesale / B2B Distribution

Wholesale / B2B Distribution

Goods sold in bulk to businesses, distributors, or retailers. Volume-driven, relationship-based. Examples: manufacturing supply chains, commodity trading, construction materials.

Service delivery

09 — In-person / On-site

In-person / On-site Service

Service delivered physically at the client's location or a dedicated venue. Examples: construction, legal advice, medical care, consulting on-site.

10 — Remote / Virtual

Remote / Virtual Service

Service delivered entirely online — video, async, or digital tools. Lower cost, global reach. Examples: remote legal, virtual advisory, online coaching.

11 — Platform / Ecosystem

Platform / Ecosystem Delivery

Core product or infrastructure extended by third-party developers, sellers, or service providers. Value scales through the ecosystem. Examples: App Store, Shopify, AWS Marketplace.

12 — Hybrid (Phygital)

Hybrid / Phygital

Intentional blend of digital and physical delivery. Not a fallback — a structural choice for businesses that serve multiple access needs. Examples: Microsoft 365, Nike (D2C + retail + app), WeWork.
