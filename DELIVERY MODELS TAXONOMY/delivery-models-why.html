Foundation

Delivery / Operating Models

How value is delivered — not how money is made. Universal across digital, physical, and service businesses.

Executive Statement

Delivery Models describe how value reaches the user. They apply equally to a SaaS startup, a Christmas ornament manufacturer, a law firm, and a Bitcoin fund.

Every company in the world delivers value through one or more Delivery / Operating Models. These models are structural, repeatable across industries, and independent of business systems and markets.

What a Delivery Model is

A Delivery Model defines:

  • how the customer accesses the product or service,
  • how the provider operates and maintains it,
  • how value is used, consumed, or experienced.

It answers "how value is delivered" — not:

  • where the company operates (market),
  • why customers pay (problem),
  • or how revenue is generated (business system).

Why the number is finite

Across industrial history, software evolution, enterprise IT, physical logistics, and service industries, value has always been delivered through a finite set of operational patterns.

New technologies change implementation, not structure. E-commerce did not create a new delivery model — it modernized Direct Physical. SaaS did not create a new category — it is a more efficient form of software delivery.

The 12 Universal Delivery / Operating Models

  • 01SaaS / Cloud Software — multi-tenant software accessed via browser
  • 02API-First / Embedded Digital — value consumed programmatically, UI optional
  • 03On-Premise / Installed Software — deployed in the customer's own environment
  • 04Open-Core + Hosted — open-source core with commercial hosting or enterprise layers
  • 05Direct Physical / D2C — goods shipped directly to the end customer
  • 06Retail / In-store — products sold through physical retail locations
  • 07On-demand / Last-mile — rapid physical delivery triggered by real-time order
  • 08Wholesale / B2B Distribution — goods sold in bulk to businesses or distributors
  • 09In-person / On-site Service — service delivered physically at client location or venue
  • 10Remote / Virtual Service — service delivered entirely online
  • 11Platform / Ecosystem Delivery — infrastructure extended by third parties
  • 12Hybrid / Phygital — intentional blend of digital and physical delivery

Hybrid is not a shortcut. It exists because real businesses at scale rarely deliver value through a single form.

Relationship to Business Systems

Business Systems

How money enters the firm.
e.g. Service / Retainer

Delivery Models

How value reaches the user.
e.g. In-person / On-site

Example: a law firm uses Service / Retainer (Business System) + In-person / On-site (Delivery Model). A remote legal platform uses the same Business System with Remote / Virtual delivery. Same system, different model.

Final rule (non-negotiable)

If someone says "we ship products" — that is a Delivery Model (Direct Physical or Wholesale).

If someone says "we are a SaaS" — that is a Delivery Model (SaaS / Cloud).

The business exists only when a Business System is also specified.
